HYMN CXTII. INDRA.

तमस्यtamasya दयावाप्र्थिवीdyāvāpṛthivī सचेतसाsacetasā विश्वेभिर्देवैरनुशुष्ममावतामviśvebhirdevairanuśuṣmamāvatām |

THE Heavens and the Earth accordant with all Gods encouraged graciously that vigorous might of his.
When he came showing forth his majesty and power, he drank of Soma juice and waxed exceeding strong.

This majesty of his Viṣṇu extols and lauds, making the stalCthat gives the meath flow forth with inight.
When Indra Maghavan with those who followed him had smitten Vṛtra he deserved the choice of Gods.

वर्त्रेणvṛtreṇa यदहिनाyadahinā बिभ्रदायुधाbibhradāyudhā समस्थिथाsamasthithā युधयेशंसमाविदेyudhayeśaṃsamāvide |

When, bearing warlike weapons, fain to win thee praise, thou mettest Vṛtra, yea, the Dragon, for the fight,
Then all the Maruts who were gathered with dice there extolled, O Mighty One, thy powerful majesty.

जज्ञानjajñāna एवeva वयबाधतvyabādhata सप्र्धःspṛdhaḥ परापश्यदprāpaśyad वीरोvīro अभिपौंस्यंabhipauṃsyaṃ रणमraṇam |

Soon as he sprang to life he forced asun. der hosts: forward the Hero looked to manly deed and war.
He cleft the rock, he let concurrent streams flow forth, and with his skilful art stablished the heavens' wide vault.
10.113.4
आदिन्द्रःādindraḥ सत्राsatrā तविषीरपत्यतtaviṣīrapatyata वरीयोvarīyo दयावाप्र्थिवीबाधतdyāvāpṛthivīabādhata |

अवाभरदavābharad धर्षितोdhṛṣito वज्रमायसंvajramāyasaṃ शेवंśevaṃ मित्रायवरुणायmitrāyavaruṇāya दाशुषेdāśuṣe ||
Indra hath evermore possessed surpassing power: he forced, far from each other, heaven and earth apart.
He hurled impetuous down his iron thunderbolt, a joy to Varuṇa's and Mitra's worshipper.
10.113.5
इन्द्रस्यात्रindrasyātra तविषीभ्योtaviṣībhyo विरप्शिनvirapśina रघायतोṛghāyato अरंहयन्तमन्यवेaraṃhayantamanyave |

वर्त्रंvṛtraṃ यदुग्रोyadughro वयव्र्श्चदोजसापोvyavṛścadojasāpo बिभ्रतन्तमसाbibhratantamasā परीव्र्तमparīvṛtam ||
Then to the mighty powers of Indra, to his wrath, his the fierce Stormer, loud of voice, they came with speed;
What time the Potent One rent Vṛtra with his strength, who held the waters back, whom darkness compassed round.
10.113.6
याyā वीर्याणिvīryāṇi परथमानिprathamāni कर्त्वाkartvā महित्वेभिर्यतमानौसमीयतुःmahitvebhiryatamānausamīyatuḥ |

धवान्तंdhvāntaṃ तमो.अवtamo.ava दध्वसेdadhvase हतhata इन्द्रोindro मह्नापूर्वहूतावपत्यतmahnāpūrvahūtāvapatyata ||
Even in the first of those heroic acts which they who strove together came with might to execute,
Deep darkness fell upon the slain, and Indra won by victory the right of being first invoked.
10.113.7

Then all the Gods extolled, with eloquence inspired by draughts of Soma juice, thy deeds of manly might.
As Agni eats the dry food with his tcetlv, he ate Vṛtra, the Dragon, maimed by Indra's deadly dart.
10.113.8
भूरिbhūri दक्षेभिर्वचनेभिरकवभिःdakṣebhirvacanebhirkvabhiḥ सख्येभिःsakhyebhiḥ सख्यानिप्रsakhyānipra वोचतvocata |

इन्द्रोindro धुनिंdhuniṃ चca चुमुरिंcumuriṃ चca दम्भयञ्छ्रद्धामनस्याdambhayañchraddhāmanasyā शर्णुतेśṛṇute दभीतयेdabhītaye ||
Proclaim his many friendships, met with friendship, made with singers, with the skilful and the eloquent.
Indra, when he subdues Dhuni and Cumuri, lists to Dabhīti for his faithful spirit's sake.
10.113.9
तवंtvaṃ पुरूण्याpurūṇyā भराbharā सवश्व्याsvaśvyā येभिर्मंसैyebhirmaṃsai निवचनानिशंसनnivacanāniśaṃsan |

सुगेभिर्विश्वाsughebhirviśvā दुरिताduritā तरेमtarema विदोvido षुṣu णṇa उर्वियागाधमद्यurviyāghādhamadya ||
Give riches manifold with noble horses, to be remembered while my songs address thee.
May we by easy paths pass all our troubles: find us this day a ford wide and extensive.
