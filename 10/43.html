HYMN XLIII. INDRA.

अछाachā मma इन्द्रंindraṃ मतयःmatayaḥ सवर्विदःsvarvidaḥ सध्रीचीर्विश्वाुशतीरनूषतsadhrīcīrviśvāuśatīranūṣata |

परिpari षवजन्तेṣvajante जनयोjanayo यथाyathā पतिंpatiṃ मर्यंनmaryaṃna शुन्ध्युंśundhyuṃ मघवानमूतयेmaghavānamūtaye ||
IN perfect unison all yearning hymns of mine that find the light of heaven have sung forth Indra's praise.
As wives embrace their lord, the comely bridegroom, so they compass Maghavan about that he may help.
10.43.1
नna घाghā तवद्रिगपtvadrighapa वेतिveti मेme मनस्त्वेmanastve इतit कामंkāmaṃ पुरुहूतशिश्रयpuruhūtaśiśraya |

राजेवrājeva दस्मdasma निni षदो.अधिṣado.adhi बर्हिष्यस्मिनbarhiṣyasmin सुsu सोमेऽवपानमस्तुsome'vapānamastu तेte ||
Directed unto thee my spirit never strays, for I have set my hopes on thee, O Much-invoked!
Sit, Wonderful! as King upon the sacred grass, and let thy drinking-place be by the Soma juice.
10.43.2
विषूव्र्दिन्द्रोviṣūvṛdindro अमतेरुतamateruta कषुधःkṣudhaḥ सsa इदid रायोrāyo मघवावस्वmaghavāvasva ईशतेīśate |

तस्येदिमेtasyedime परवणेpravaṇe सप्तsapta सिन्धवोsindhavo वयोवर्धन्तिvayovardhanti वर्षभस्यvṛṣabhasya शुष्मिणःśuṣmiṇaḥ ||
From indigence and hunger Indra turns away: Maghavan hath dominion over precious wealth.
These the Seven Rivers flowing on their downward path increase the vital vigour of the potent Steer.
10.43.3
वयोvayo नna वर्क्षंvṛkṣaṃ सुपलाशमासदनsupalāśamāsadan सोमासsomāsa इन्द्रंindraṃ मन्दिनश्चमूषदःmandinaścamūṣadaḥ |

परैषामनीकंpraiṣāmanīkaṃ शवसाśavasā दविद्युतदdavidyutad विदत्स्वर्मनवेvidatsvarmanave जयोतिरार्यमjyotirāryam ||
As on the fair-leafed tree rest birds, to Indra flow the gladdening Soma juices that the bowls contain.
Their face that glows with splendour through their mighty power hath found the shine of heaven for man, the Āryas' light.
10.43.4
कर्तंkṛtaṃ नna शवघ्नीśvaghnī विvi चिनोतिcinoti देवनेdevane संवर्गंsaṃvarghaṃ यनyan मघवासूर्यंmaghavāsūryaṃ जयतjayat |

नna ततtat तेte अन्योanyo अनुanu वीर्यंvīryaṃ शकनśakan नपुराणोnapurāṇo मघवनmaghavan नोतnota नूतनःnūtanaḥ ||
As in the game a gambler piles his winnings, so Maghavan, sweeping all together, gained the Sun
This mighty deed of thine none other could achieve, none, Maghavan, before thee, none in recent time.
10.43.5
विशं-विशंviśaṃ-viśaṃ मघवाmaghavā पर्यशायतparyaśāyata जनानांjanānāṃ धेनावचाकशदdhenāavacākaśad वर्षाvṛṣā |

यस्याहyasyāha शक्रःśakraḥ सवनेषुsavaneṣu रण्यतिraṇyati सतीव्रैःsatīvraiḥ सोमैःsomaiḥ सहतेsahate पर्तन्यतःpṛtanyataḥ ||
Maghavan came by turns to all the tribes of men: the Steer took notice of the people's songs of praise.
The man in whose libations Śakra hath delight by means of potent Somas vanquisheth his foes.
10.43.6
आपोāpo नna सिन्धुमभिsindhumabhi यतyat समक्षरनsamakṣaran सोमासsomāsa इन्द्रंindraṃ कुल्यािवkulyāiva हरदमhradam |

वर्धन्तिvardhanti विप्राviprā महोmaho अस्यasya सादनेsādane यवंyavaṃ नव्र्ष्टिर्दिव्येनnavṛṣṭirdivyena दानुनाdānunā ||
When Soma streams together unto Indra flow like waters to the river, rivulets to the lake,
In place of sacrifice sages exalt his might, as the rain swells the corn by moisture sent from heaven.
10.43.7
वर्षाvṛṣā नna करुद्धःkruddhaḥ पतयदpatayad रजस्स्वाrajassvā योyo अर्यपत्नीरक्र्णोदिमाaryapatnīrakṛṇodimā अपःapaḥ |

सsa सुन्वतेsunvate मघवाmaghavā जीरदानवे.अविन्दज्ज्योतिर्मनवेjīradānave.avindajjyotirmanave हविष्मतेhaviṣmate ||
He rushes through the region like a furious Bull, he who hath made these floods the dames of worthy lords.
This Maghavan hath found light for the man who brings oblation, sheds the juice, and promptly pours his gifts.
10.43.8
उज्जायतांujjāyatāṃ परशुर्ज्योतिषाparaśurjyotiṣā सहsaha भूयाbhūyā रतस्यṛtasya सुदुघापुराणवतsudughāpurāṇavat |

विvi रोचतामरुषोrocatāmaruṣo भानुनाbhānunā शुचिःśuciḥ सवर्णशुक्रंsvarṇaśukraṃ शुशुचीतśuśucīta सत्पतिःsatpatiḥ ||
Let the keen axe come forth together with the light: here be,.as erst, the teeming cow of sacrifice.
Let the Red God shine bright with his refulgent ray, and let the Lord of heroes glow like heaven's clear sheen.
10.43.9
गोभिषghobhiṣ टरेमामतिंṭaremāmatiṃ ...... ||
O Much-invoked, may we subdue all famine and evil want with store of grain and cattle.
May we allied, as first in rank, with princes obtain possessions by our own exertion.
10.43.10
बर्हस्पतिर्नःbṛhaspatirnaḥ परिpari ...... ||
Bṛhaspati protect us from the rearward, and from above, and from below, from sinners.
May Indra from the front, and from the centre, as Friend to friends, vouchsafe us room and freedom.
