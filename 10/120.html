HYMN CXX. INDRA.

तदिदासtadidāsa भुवनेषुbhuvaneṣu जयेष्ठंjyeṣṭhaṃ यतोyato जज्ञjajña उग्रस्त्वेषन्र्म्णःughrastveṣanṛmṇaḥ |

सद्योsadyo जज्ञानोjajñāno निni रिणातिriṇāti शत्रूननुśatrūnanu यंविश्वेyaṃviśve मदन्त्यूमाःmadantyūmāḥ ||
IN all the worlds That was the Best and Highest whence sprang the Mighty Gods, of splendid valour.
As soon as born he overcomes his foemen, be in whom all who lend him aid are joyful.
10.120.1
वाव्र्धानःvāvṛdhānaḥ शवसाśavasā भूर्योजाःbhūryojāḥ शत्रुर्दासायśatrurdāsāya भियसन्दधातिbhiyasandadhāti |

अव्यनच्चavyanacca वयनच्चvyanacca सस्निsasni संsaṃ तेte नवन्तnavanta परभ्र्तामदेषुprabhṛtāmadeṣu ||
Grown mighty in his strength, with ample vigour, he as a foe strikes fear into the Dāsa,
Eager to win the breathing and the breathless. All sang thy praise at banquet and oblation.
10.120.2
तवेtve करतुमपिkratumapi वर्ञ्जन्तिvṛñjanti विश्वेviśve दविर्यदेतेdviryadete तरिर्भवन्त्यूमाःtrirbhavantyūmāḥ |

सवादोःsvādoḥ सवादीयःsvādīyaḥ सवादुनाsvādunā सर्जाsṛjā समदःsamadaḥ सुमधुsumadhu मधुनाभिmadhunābhi योधीःyodhīḥ ||
All concentrate on thee their mental vigour, what time these, twice or thrice, are thine assistants.
Blend what is sweeter than the sweet with sweetness: win. quickly with our meath that meath in battle.
10.120.3
इतिiti चिदcid धिdhi तवाtvā धनाdhanā जयन्तंjayantaṃ मदे-मदेmade-made अनुमदन्तिanumadanti विप्राःviprāḥ |

ओजीयोojīyo धर्ष्णोdhṛṣṇo सथिरमाsthiramā तनुष्वtanuṣva माmā तवाtvā दभन्यातुधानाdabhanyātudhānā दुरेवाःdurevāḥ ||
Therefore in thee too, thou who winnest riches, at every banquet are the sages joyful.
With mightier power, Bold God, extend thy firmness: let not malignant Yātudhānas harm thee.
10.120.4
तवयाtvayā वयंvayaṃ शाशद्महेśāśadmahe रणेषुraṇeṣu परपश्यन्तोprapaśyanto युधेन्यानिभूरिyudhenyānibhūri |

चोदयामिcodayāmi तta आयुधाāyudhā वचोभिःvacobhiḥ संsaṃ तेte शिशामिब्रह्मणाśiśāmibrahmaṇā वयांसिvayāṃsi ||
Proudly we put our trust in thee in battles, when we behold great wealth the prize of combat.
I with my words impel thy weapons onward, and sharpen with my prayer thy vital vigour.
10.120.5
सतुषेय्यंstuṣeyyaṃ पुरुवर्पसंpuruvarpasaṃ रभ्वमिनतममाप्त्यमाप्त्यानामṛbhvaminatamamāptyamāptyānām |

आā दर्षतेdarṣate शवसाśavasā सप्तsapta दानूनdānūn परpra साक्षतेsākṣate परतिमानानिभूरिpratimānānibhūri ||
Worthy of praises, many-shaped, most skilful, most energetic, Āptya of the Aptyas:
He with his might destroys the seven Danus, subduing many who were deemed his equals.
10.120.6
निni तदtad दधिषे.अवरंdadhiṣe.avaraṃ परंparaṃ चca यस्मिन्नाविथावसाyasminnāvithāvasā दुरोणेduroṇe |

आā मातराmātarā सथापयसेsthāpayase जिगत्नूjighatnū अतata इनोषिinoṣi कर्वराkarvarā पुरूणिpurūṇi ||
Thou in that house which thy protection guardeth bestowest wealth, the higher and the lower.
Thou stablishest the two much-wandering Mothers, and bringest many deeds to their completion.
10.120.7
इमाimā बरह्मbrahma बर्हद्दिवोbṛhaddivo विवक्तीन्द्रायvivaktīndrāya शूषमग्रियःस्वर्षाःśūṣamaghriyaḥsvarṣāḥ |

महोmaho गोत्रस्यghotrasya कषयतिkṣayati सवराजोsvarājo दुरश्चduraśca विश्वाव्र्णोदपviśvāavṛṇodapa सवाःsvāḥ ||
Brhaddiva, the foremost of light-winners, repeats these holy prayers, this strength of Indra.
He rules the great self-luminous fold of cattle, and all the doors of light hath he thrown open.
10.120.8
एवाevā महानmahān बर्हद्दिवोbṛhaddivo अथर्वावोचतatharvāvocat सवांsvāṃ तन्वमिन्द्रमेवtanvamindrameva |

सवसारोsvasāro मातरिभ्वरीररिप्राmātaribhvarīrariprā हिन्वन्तिhinvanti चca शवसावर्धयन्तिśavasāvardhayanti चca ||
Thus hath Brhaddiva, the great Atharvan, spoken to Indra as himself in person.
The spotless Sisters, they who are his Mothers, with power exalt him and impel him onward.
10.120.9
