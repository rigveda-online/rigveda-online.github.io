HYMN XLIV. INDRA.

योyo रयिवोrayivo रयिन्तमोrayintamo योyo दयुम्नैर्द्युम्नवत्तमःdyumnairdyumnavattamaḥ |

सोमःsomaḥ सुतःsutaḥ सsa इन्द्रindra ते.अस्तिte.asti सवधापतेsvadhāpate मदःmadaḥ ||
THAT which is wealthiest, Wealthy God in splendoursmost illustrious,
Soma is pressed: thy gladdening draught, Indra! libation's Lord! is this.
6.44.1
यःyaḥ शग्मस्तुविशग्मśaghmastuviśaghma तेte रायोrāyo दामाdāmā मतीनामmatīnām |

सोमःsomaḥ सुतःsutaḥ ...... ||
Effectual, Most Effectual One! thine, as bestowing wealth of hymns,
Soma is pressed: thy gladdening draught, Indra! libation's Lord! is this.
6.44.2
येनyena वर्द्धोvṛddho नna शवसाśavasā तुरोturo नna सवाभिरूतिभिःsvābhirūtibhiḥ |

सोमःsomaḥ सुतःsutaḥ ...... ||
Wherewith thou art increased in strength, and conquerest with thy proper aids,
Soma is pressed: thy gladdening draught, Indra! libation's Lord! is this.
6.44.3
तयमुtyamu वोvo अप्रहणंaprahaṇaṃ गर्णीषेghṛṇīṣe शवससśavasas पतिमpatim |

इन्द्रंindraṃ विश्वासाहंviśvāsāhaṃ नरंnaraṃ मंहिष्ठंmaṃhiṣṭhaṃ विश्वचर्षणिमviśvacarṣaṇim ||
Him for your sake I glorify as Lord of Strength who wrongeth none,
The Hero Indra, conquering all, Most Bounteous, God of all the tribes.
6.44.4
यंyaṃ वर्धयन्तीदvardhayantīd गिरःghiraḥ पतिंpatiṃ तुरस्यturasya राधसःrādhasaḥ |

तमिन्न्वस्यtaminnvasya रोदसीrodasī देवीdevī शुष्मंśuṣmaṃ सपर्यतःsaparyataḥ ||
Those Goddesses, both Heaven and Earth, revere the power and might of him,
Him whom our songs increase in strength, the Lord of bounty swift to come.
6.44.5
तदtad वva उक्थस्यukthasya बर्हणेन्द्रायोपस्त्र्णीषणिbarhaṇendrāyopastṛṇīṣaṇi |

विपोvipo नna यस्योतयोyasyotayo विvi यदyad रोहन्तिrohanti सक्षितःsakṣitaḥ ||
To seat your Indra, I will spread abroad with power this song of praise.
The saving succours that abide in him, like songs, extend and grow.
6.44.6
अविददavidad दक्षंdakṣaṃ मित्रोmitro नवीयानnavīyān पपानोpapāno देवेभ्योdevebhyo वस्योvasyo अचैतacait |

ससवानsasavān सतौलाभिर्धौतरीभिरुरुष्याstaulābhirdhautarībhiruruṣyā पायुरभवतpāyurabhavat सखिभ्यःsakhibhyaḥ ||
A recent Friend, he found the skilful priest: he drank, and showed forth treasure from the Gods.
He conquered, borne by strong all-shaking mares, and was with far-spread power his friends' Protector.
6.44.7
रतस्यṛtasya पथिpathi वेधाvedhā अपायिapāyi शरियेśriye मनांसिmanāṃsi देवासोdevāso अक्रनakran |

दधानोdadhāno नामnāma महोmaho वचोभिर्वपुर्द्र्शयेvacobhirvapurdṛśaye वेन्योvenyo वयावःvyāvaḥ ||
In course of Law the sapient juice was quaffed: the Deities to glory turned their mind.
Winning through hymns a lofty title, he, the Lovely, made his beauteous form apparent.
6.44.8
दयुमत्तमंdyumattamaṃ दक्षंdakṣaṃ धेह्यस्मेdhehyasme सेधाsedhā जनानांjanānāṃ पूर्वीररातीःpūrvīrarātīḥ |

वर्षीयोvarṣīyo वयःvayaḥ कर्णुहिkṛṇuhi शचीभिर्धनस्यśacībhirdhanasya सातावस्मानविड्ढिsātāvasmānaviḍḍhi ||
Bestow on us the most illustrious strength ward off men's manifold malignities.
Give with thy might abundant vital force, and aid us graciously in gaining riches.
6.44.9
इन्द्रindra तुभ्यमिनtubhyamin मघवन्नभूमmaghavannabhūma वयंvayaṃ दात्रेdātre हरिवोharivo माmā विवेनःvivenaḥ |

नकिरापिर्दद्र्शेnakirāpirdadṛśe मर्त्यत्राmartyatrā किमङगkimaṅgha रध्रचोदनन्त्वाहुःradhracodanantvāhuḥ ||
We turn to thee as Giver, liberal Indra. Lord of the Bay Steeds, be not thou ungracious.
No friend among mankind have we to lookto: why have men called thee him who spurs the niggard?
6.44.10
माmā जस्वनेjasvane वर्षभvṛṣabha नोno ररीथाrarīthā माmā तेte रेवतःrevataḥ सख्येsakhye रिषामriṣāma |

पूर्वीषpūrvīṣ टṭa इन्द्रindra निष्षिधोniṣṣidho जनेषुjaneṣu जह्यसुष्वीनjahyasuṣvīn परpra वर्हाप्र्णतःvṛhāpṛṇataḥ ||
Give us not up, Strong Hero! to the hungry: unharmed be we whom thou, so rich, befriendest.
Full many a boon hast thou for men demolish those who present no gifts nor pour oblations.
6.44.11
उदभ्राणीवudabhrāṇīva सतनयन्नियर्तीन्द्रोstanayanniyartīndro राधांस्यश्व्यानिrādhāṃsyaśvyāni गव्याghavyā |

तवमसिtvamasi परदिवःpradivaḥ कारुधायाkārudhāyā माmā तवादामानtvādāmāna आā दभन्मघोनःdabhanmaghonaḥ ||
As Indra thundering impels the rain-clouds, so doth he send us store of kine and horses.
Thou art of old the Cherisher of singers let not the rich who bring no gifts deceive thee.
6.44.12
अध्वर्योadhvaryo वीरvīra परpra महेmahe सुतानामिन्द्रायsutānāmindrāya भरbhara सsa हयस्यhyasya राजाrājā |

यःyaḥ पूर्व्याभिरुतpūrvyābhiruta नूतनाभिर्गीर्भिर्वाव्र्धेnūtanābhirghīrbhirvāvṛdhe गर्णतांghṛṇatāṃ रषीणामṛṣīṇām ||
Adbyaryu, hero, bring to mighty Indrafor he is King thereof-the pressed-out juices;
To him exalted by the hymns and praises, ancient and modern, of the singing Ṛṣis.
6.44.13
अस्यasya मदेmade पुरुpuru वर्पांसिvarpāṃsi विद्वानिन्द्रोvidvānindro वर्त्राण्यप्रतीvṛtrāṇyapratī जघानjaghāna |

तमुtamu परpra होषिhoṣi मधुमन्तमस्मैmadhumantamasmai सोमंsomaṃ वीरायvīrāya शिप्रिणेśipriṇe पिबध्यैpibadhyai ||
In the wild joy of this hath Indra, knowing full many a form, struck down resistless Vṛtras.
Proclaim aloud to him the savoury Soma so that the Hero, strong of jaw, may drink it.
6.44.14
पाताpātā सुतमिन्द्रोsutamindro अस्तुastu सोमंsomaṃ हन्ताhantā वर्त्रंvṛtraṃ वज्रेणvajreṇa मन्दसानःmandasānaḥ |

गन्ताghantā यज्ञंyajñaṃ परावतश्चिदछाparāvataścidachā वसुर्धीनामविताvasurdhīnāmavitā कारुधायाःkārudhāyāḥ ||
May Indra drink this Soma poured to please him, and cheered therewith slay Vṛtra with his thunder.
Come to our sacrifice even from a distance, good lover of our songs, the bard's Supporter.
6.44.15
इदंidaṃ तयतtyat पात्रमिन्द्रपानमिन्द्रस्यpātramindrapānamindrasya परियमम्र्तमपायिpriyamamṛtamapāyi |

मत्सदmatsad यथाyathā सौमनसायsaumanasāya देवंdevaṃ वयस्मदvyasmad दवेषोdveṣo युयवदyuyavad वयंहःvyaṃhaḥ ||
The cup whence Indra drinks the draught is present: the Amṛta dear to Indra hath been drunken,
That it may cheer the God to gracious favour, and keep far from us hatred and affliction.
6.44.16
एनाenā मन्दानोmandāno जहिjahi शूरśūra शत्रूञśatrūñ जामिमजामिंjāmimajāmiṃ मघवन्नमित्रानmaghavannamitrān |

अभिषेणानभ्यादेदिशानानabhiṣeṇānabhyādediśānān पराचparāca इन्द्रindra परpra मर्णाजहीmṛṇājahī चca ||
Therewith enraptured, Hero, slay our foemen, the unfriendly, Maghavan be they kin or strangers,
Those who still aim their hostile darts to smite us, turn them to flight, O Indra, crush and kill them.
6.44.17
आसुāsu षमाṣmā णोṇo मघवन्निन्द्रmaghavannindra पर्त्स्वस्मभ्यंpṛtsvasmabhyaṃ महिmahi वरिवःvarivaḥ सुगंsughaṃ कःkaḥ |

अपांapāṃ तोकस्यtokasya तनयस्यtanayasya जेषjeṣa इन्द्रindra सूरीनsūrīn कर्णुहिस्माkṛṇuhismā नोno अर्धमardham ||
O Indra Maghavan, in these our battles win easy paths for us and ample freedom.
That we may gain waters and seed and offspring, set thou our princes on thy side, O Indra.
6.44.18
आā तवाtvā हरयोharayo वर्षणोvṛṣaṇo युजानाyujānā वर्षरथासोvṛṣarathāso वर्षरश्मयो.अत्याःvṛṣaraśmayo.atyāḥ |

अस्मत्राञ्चोasmatrāñco वर्षणोvṛṣaṇo वज्रवाहोvajravāho वर्ष्णेvṛṣṇe मदायmadāya सुयुजोवहन्तुsuyujovahantu ||
Let thy Bay Stallions, harnessed, bring thee hither, Steeds with strong chariot and strong reins to hold them,
Strong Horses, speeding hither, bearing thunder, well-harnessed, for the strong exciting potion.
6.44.19
आā तेte वर्षनvṛṣan वर्षणोvṛṣaṇo दरोणमस्थुर्घ्र्तप्रुषोdroṇamasthurghṛtapruṣo नोर्मयोnormayo मदन्तःmadantaḥ |

इन्द्रindra परpra तुभ्यंtubhyaṃ वर्षभिःvṛṣabhiḥ सुतानांsutānāṃ वर्ष्णेvṛṣṇe भरन्तिव्र्षभायbharantivṛṣabhāya सोममsomam ||
Beside the vat, Strong God! stand thy strong Horses, shining with holy oil, like waves exulting.
Indra, they bring to thee, the Strong and Mighty, Soma of juices shed by mighty press-stones.
6.44.20
वर्षासिvṛṣāsi दिवोdivo वर्षभःvṛṣabhaḥ पर्थिव्याpṛthivyā वर्षाvṛṣā सिन्धूनांsindhūnāṃ वर्षभस्तियानामvṛṣabhastiyānām |

वर्ष्णेvṛṣṇe तta इन्दुर्व्र्षभindurvṛṣabha पीपायpīpāya सवादूsvādū रसोraso मधुपेयोmadhupeyo वरायvarāya ||
Thou art the Bull of earth, the Bull of heaven, Bull of the rivers, Bull of standing waters.
For thee, the Strong, O Bull, hath Indu swollen. juice pleasant, sweet to drink, for thine election.
6.44.21
अयंayaṃ देवःdevaḥ सहसाsahasā जायमानjāyamāna इन्द्रेणindreṇa युजाyujā पणिमस्तभायतpaṇimastabhāyat |

अयंayaṃ सवस्यsvasya पितुरायुधानीन्दुरमुष्णादशिवस्यpiturāyudhānīnduramuṣṇādaśivasya मायाःmāyāḥ ||
This God, with might, when first he had his being, with Indra for ally, held fast the Paṇi.
This Indu stole away the warlike weapons, and foiled the arts of his malignant father.
6.44.22
अयमक्र्णोदुषसःayamakṛṇoduṣasaḥ सुपत्नीरयंsupatnīrayaṃ सूर्येsūrye अदधाज्ज्योतिरन्तःadadhājjyotirantaḥ |

अयंayaṃ तरिधातुtridhātu दिविdivi रोचनेषुrocaneṣu तरितेषुtriteṣu विन्ददम्र्तंvindadamṛtaṃ निगूळ्हमnighūḷham ||
The Dawns he wedded to a glorious Consort, and set within the Sun the light that lights him.
He found in heaven, in the third lucid regions, the threefold Amṛta in its close concealment.
6.44.23
अयंayaṃ दयावाप्र्थिवीdyāvāpṛthivī विvi षकभायदयंṣkabhāyadayaṃ रथमयुनकrathamayunak सप्तरश्मिमsaptaraśmim |

अयंayaṃ गोषुghoṣu शच्याśacyā पक्वमन्तःpakvamantaḥ सोमोsomo दाधारdādhāra दशयन्त्रमुत्समdaśayantramutsam ||
He stayed and held the heaven and earth asunder: the chariot with the sevenfold reins he harnessed.
This Soma Set with power within the milch-kine a spring whose ripe contents ten fingers empty.
6.44.24
