HYMN XXXIX INDRA.

मन्द्रस्यmandrasya कवेर्दिव्यस्यkaverdivyasya वह्नेर्विप्रमन्मनोvahnervipramanmano वचनस्यvacanasya मध्वःmadhvaḥ |

अपाapā नस्तस्यnastasya सचनस्यsacanasya देवेषोdeveṣo युवस्वyuvasva गर्णतेghṛṇate गोग्राःghoaghrāḥ ||
OF this our charming, our celestial Soma, eloquent, wise, Priest, with inspired devotion,
Of this thy close attendant, hast thou drunken. God, send the singer food with milk to grace it.
6.39.1
अयमुशानःayamuśānaḥ पर्यद्रिमुस्रparyadrimusra रतधीतिभिर्र्तयुगṛtadhītibhirṛtayugh युजानःyujānaḥ |

रुजदरुग्णंrujadarughṇaṃ विvi वलस्यvalasya सानुंsānuṃ पणीन्र्वचोभिरभिpaṇīnrvacobhirabhi योधदिन्द्रःyodhadindraḥ ||
Craving the kine, rushing against the mountain led on by Law, with holyminded comrades,
He broke the never-broken ridge of Vala. With words of might Indra subdued the Paṇis.
6.39.2
अयंayaṃ दयोतयदद्युतोdyotayadadyuto वयक्तूनvyaktūn दोषाdoṣā वस्तोःvastoḥ शरदśarada इन्दुरिन्द्रindurindra |

इमंimaṃ केतुमदधुर्नूketumadadhurnū चिदह्नांcidahnāṃ शुचिजन्मनśucijanmana उषसश्चकारuṣasaścakāra ||
This Indu lighted darksome nights, O Indra, throughout the years, at morning and at evening.
Him have they stablished as the days' bright ensign. He made the Mornings to be born in splendour.
6.39.3
अयंayaṃ रोचयदरुचोrocayadaruco रुचानो.अयंrucāno.ayaṃ वासयदvāsayad वयvy रतेनṛtena पूर्वीःpūrvīḥ |

अयमीयतayamīyata रतयुग्भिरश्वैःṛtayughbhiraśvaiḥ सवर्विदाsvarvidā नाभिनाnābhinā चर्षणिप्राःcarṣaṇiprāḥ ||
He shone and caused to shme the worlds that shone not. By Law he lighted up the host of Mornings.
He moves with Steeds yoked by eternal Order, contenting men with nave that finds the sunlight.
6.39.4
नूnū गर्णानोghṛṇāno गर्णतेghṛṇate परत्नpratna राजन्निषःrājanniṣaḥ पिन्वpinva वसुदेयायvasudeyāya पूर्वीःpūrvīḥ |

अपapa ओषधीरविषाoṣadhīraviṣā वनानिvanāni गाghā अर्वतोarvato नॄनnṝn रचसेṛcase रिरीहिrirīhi ||
Now, praised, O Ancient King! fill thou the singer with plenteous food that he may deal forth treasures.
Give waters, herbs that have no poison, forests, and kine, and steeds, and men, to him who lauds thee.
