HYMN LXVI. INDRA.

तरोभिर्वोtarobhirvo विदद्वसुमिन्द्रंvidadvasumindraṃ सबाधsabādha ऊतयेūtaye |

बर्हदbṛhad गायन्तःghāyantaḥ सुतसोमेsutasome अध्वरेadhvare हुवेhuve भरंbharaṃ नna कारिणमkāriṇam ||
Thronging together, (worship) for your protection Indra full of might and the revealer of wealth; (worship him), chanting the Bṛhat-sāman at his sacrifice where the Soma is effused, I invoke him as (men invoke) abeneficent master of a household.
8.66.1
नna यंyaṃ दुध्राdudhrā वरन्तेvarante नna सथिराsthirā मुरोmuro मदेmade सुषिप्रमन्धसःsuṣipramandhasaḥ |

यya आद्र्त्याādṛtyā शशमानायśaśamānāya सुन्वतेsunvate दाताdātā जरित्रjaritra उक्थ्यमukthyam ||
He, the handsome-jawed, whom, inthe intoxication of the Soma, the fierce (demons) withstand not, north firm gods, nor mortal (men)-- who confers glorious wealth on him who reverently praises him, offers the Soma and sings hymns.
8.66.2
यःyaḥ शक्रोśakro मर्क्षोmṛkṣo अश्व्योaśvyo योyo वाvā कीजोkījo हिरण्ययःhiraṇyayaḥ |

सsa ऊर्वस्यūrvasya रेजयत्यपाव्र्तिमिन्द्रोrejayatyapāvṛtimindro गव्यस्यghavyasya वर्त्रहाvṛtrahā ||
He Śakra, who is the purifier (of his worshippers), and well-skilled in horses, who is wonderful andgolden-bodied-- he, Indra, the slayer of Vṛtra, shakes the hiding-plural ce of the numerous herd of kine.
8.66.3
निखातंnikhātaṃ चिदcid यःyaḥ पुरुसम्भ्र्तंpurusambhṛtaṃ वसूदिदvasūdid वपतिvapati दाशुषेdāśuṣe |

वज्रीvajrī सुशिप्रोsuśipro हर्यश्वharyaśva इतit करदिन्द्रःkaradindraḥ करत्वाkratvā यथाyathā वशतvaśat ||
He who verily pours forth to the offerer the buried wealth accusative ulated by many, he, Indra, thethunderer, handsome-jawed, borne on bay steeds, does as he plural ases, (when propitiated) with sacrifice.
8.66.4
यदyad वावन्थvāvantha पुरुष्टुतpuruṣṭuta पुराpurā चिच्छूरcicchūra नर्णामnṛṇām |

वयंvayaṃ तत्तtatta इन्द्रindra संsaṃ भरामसिbharāmasi यज्ञमुक्थंyajñamukthaṃ तुरंturaṃ वचःvacaḥ ||
Hero, praised of many, what of old time you did desire from your votaries, that, Indra, we hasten to bringto you-- oblation and recited praise.
8.66.5
सचाsacā सोमेषुsomeṣu पुरुहूतpuruhūta वज्रिवोvajrivo मदायmadāya दयुक्षdyukṣa सोमपाःsomapāḥ |

तवमिदtvamid धिdhi बरह्मक्र्तेbrahmakṛte काम्यंkāmyaṃ वसुvasu देष्ठःdeṣṭhaḥ सुन्वतेsunvate भुवःbhuvaḥ ||
Bearer of the thunderbolt, invoked of many, radiant, drinker of the Soma, be present at our libations foryour exhilaration; for you are an abundant giver of desirable wealth to him who utters your praises and effusesthe Soma.
8.66.6
वयमेनमिदाvayamenamidā हयो.अपीपेमेहhyo.apīpemeha वज्रिणमvajriṇam |

तस्माtasmā उu अद्यadya समनाsamanā सुतंsutaṃ भराbharā नूनंnūnaṃ भूषतbhūṣata शरुतेśrute ||
Today and yesterday we have here refreshed him, the thunderer; bring to him today our libation effusedfor (success in) battle; let him now hasten here on hearing our praise.
8.66.7
वर्कश्चिदस्यvṛkaścidasya वारणvāraṇa उरामथिराurāmathirā वयुनेषुvayuneṣu भूषतिbhūṣati |

सेमंsemaṃ नःnaḥ सतोमंstomaṃ जुजुषाणjujuṣāṇa आā गहीन्द्रghahīndra परpra चित्रयाcitrayā धियाdhiyā ||
The obstructing robber, the destroyer of travelling enemies, is obedient to him in hiw ways; hasten,Indra, (drawn) by our gorgeous rite, welcoming this our hymn.
8.66.8
कदूkadū नवस्याक्र्तमिन्द्रस्यास्तिnvasyākṛtamindrasyāsti पौंस्यमpauṃsyam |

केनोkeno नुnu कंkaṃ शरोमतेनśromatena नna शुश्रुवेśuśruve जनुषःjanuṣaḥ परिpari वर्त्रहाvṛtrahā ||
What act of might is there, unperformed by Indra? Who has not heard of his famous (heroism)? He, theslayer of Vṛtra, (is renowned) from his birth.
8.66.9
कदूkadū महीरध्र्ष्टाmahīradhṛṣṭā अस्यasya तविषीःtaviṣīḥ कदुkadu वर्त्रघ्नोvṛtraghno अस्त्र्तमastṛtam |

इन्द्रोindro विश्वानviśvān बेकनाटानहर्द्र्शbekanāṭānahardṛśa उतuta करत्वाkratvā पणीन्रभिpaṇīnrabhi ||
When were his mighty forces ever languid? When was aught undestroyed before the slayer of Vṛtra?Indra by his energy overpowers all the huckstering usurers who see only this world's days.
8.66.10
वयंvayaṃ घाghā तेte अपूर्व्येन्द्रapūrvyendra बरह्माणिbrahmāṇi वर्त्रहनvṛtrahan |

पुरूतमासःपुरुहूतpurūtamāsaḥpuruhūta वज्रिवोvajrivo भर्तिंbhṛtiṃ नna परpra भरामसिbharāmasi ||
Indra, slayer of Vṛra, invoked of many, we, your many worshippers, offer new hymns to you, thunderer,as your wages.
8.66.11
पूर्वीश्चिदpūrvīścid धिdhi तवेtve तुविकूर्मिन्नाशसोtuvikūrminnāśaso हवन्तhavanta इन्द्रोतयःindrotayaḥ |

तिरश्चिदर्यःtiraścidaryaḥ सवनाsavanā वसोvaso गहिghahi शविष्ठśaviṣṭha शरुधिśrudhi मेme हवमhavam ||
Indra, doer of many great deeds, (other worshippers) invoke the manifold hopes and protections whichabide in you; but rejecting the enemy's oblations, come to us bestower of dwellings; O mightiest, hear my appeal.
8.66.12
वयंvayaṃ घाghā तेte तवेtve इदid विन्द्रvindra विप्रvipra अपिapi षमसिṣmasi |

नहिnahi तवदन्यःtvadanyaḥ पुरुहूतpuruhūta कश्चनkaścana मघवन्नस्तिmaghavannasti मर्डिताmarḍitā ||
Indra, we are your, therefore we, your worshippers, depend on you; other than you, Maghavan, invokedof many, there is no giver of happiness.
8.66.13
तवंtvaṃ नोno अस्याasyā अमतेरुतamateruta कषुधो.अभिशस्तेरवkṣudho.abhiśasterava सप्र्धिspṛdhi |

तवंtvaṃ नna उतीutī तवtava चित्रयाcitrayā धियाdhiyā शिक्षाśikṣā शचिष्ठśaciṣṭha गातुवितghātuvit ||
Deliver from us this poverty, hunger and calumny; give us (our desire) by your protection and wondrousworking; O mightiest, you know the right way.
8.66.14
सोमsoma इदid वःvaḥ सुतोsuto अस्तुastu कलयोkalayo माmā बिभीतनbibhītana |

अपेदेषapedeṣa धवस्मायतिdhvasmāyati सवयंsvayaṃ घैषोghaiṣo अपायतिapāyati ||
Let your effused Soma be only (for Indra); O sons of Kali, fear not; that malignant (spirit) departs, of hisown accord he departs.
