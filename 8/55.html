HYMN LV. INDRA.

भूरीदिन्द्रस्यbhūrīdindrasya वीर्यंvīryaṃ वयख्यमभ्यायतिvyakhyamabhyāyati |

राधस्तेrādhaste दस्यवेdasyave वर्कvṛka ||
LOUD singing at the sacred rite where Soma flows we priests invoke
With haste, that he may help, as the bard's Cherisher, Indra who findeth wealth for you.
8.55.1
शतंśataṃ शवेतासśvetāsa उक्षणोukṣaṇo दिविdivi तारोtāro नna रोचन्तेrocante |

मह्नाmahnā दिवंdivaṃ नna तस्तभुःtastabhuḥ ||
Whom with fair helm, in rapture of the juice, the firm resistless slayers hinder not:
Giver of glorious wealth to him who sing a his praise, honouring him who toils and pours:
8.55.2
शतंśataṃ वेणूञ्छतंveṇūñchataṃ शुनःśunaḥ शतंśataṃ चर्माणिcarmāṇi मलातानिmlātāni |

शतंśataṃ मेme बल्बजस्तुकाbalbajastukā अरुषीणांaruṣīṇāṃ चतुःशतमcatuḥśatam ||
Śakra, who like a curry-comb for horses or a golden goad,
Indra, the Vṛtra-slayer, urges eagerly the opening of the stall of kine:
8.55.3
सुदेवाःsudevāḥ सथstha काण्वायनाkāṇvāyanā वयो-वयोvayo-vayo विचरन्तःvicarantaḥ |

अश्वासोaśvāso नचङकरमतnacaṅkramata ||
Who for the worshipper scatters forth ample wealth, even though buried, piled in heaps:
May Indra, Lord of Bay Steeds, fair-helmed Thunderer, act at his pleasure, as he lists.
8.55.4
आदितādit साप्तस्यsāptasya चर्किरन्नानूनस्यcarkirannānūnasya महिmahi शरवःśravaḥ |

शयावीरतिध्वसनśyāvīratidhvasan पथश्चक्षुषाpathaścakṣuṣā चनcana संनशेsaṃnaśe ||
Hero whom many praise, what thou hast longed for, oven of old, from men.
All that we offer unto thee, O Indra, now, sacrifice, laud, effectual speech.
8.55.5
