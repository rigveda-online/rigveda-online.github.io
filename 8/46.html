HYMN XLVI. INDRA.

तवावतःtvāvataḥ पुरूवसोpurūvaso वयमिन्द्रvayamindra परणेतःpraṇetaḥ समसिsmasi सथातर्हरीणामsthātarharīṇām ||
WE, Indra, Lord of ample wealth, our Guide, depend on one like thee,
Thou driver of the Tawny Steeds.
8.46.1
तवांtvāṃ हिhi सत्यमद्रिवोsatyamadrivo विद्मvidma दातारमिषामdātāramiṣām |

विद्मvidma दातारंdātāraṃ रयीणामrayīṇām ||
For, Hurler of the Bolt, we know thee true, the giver of our food,
We know the giver of our wealth.
8.46.2
आā यस्यyasya तेte महिमानंmahimānaṃ शतमूतेśatamūte शतक्रतोśatakrato |

गीर्भिर्ग्र्णन्तिghīrbhirghṛṇanti कारवःkāravaḥ ||
O thou whose majesty the bards celebrate with their songs, thou Lord,
Of hundred powers and hundred aids.
8.46.3
सुनीथोsunītho घाghā सsa मर्त्योmartyo यंyaṃ मरुतोmaruto यमर्यमाyamaryamā |

मित्रःmitraḥ पान्त्यद्रुहःpāntyadruhaḥ ||
Fair guidance hath the mortal man whom Aryaman, the Marut host,
And Mitra, void of guile, protect.
8.46.4
दधानोdadhāno गोमदश्ववदghomadaśvavad सुवीर्यमादित्यजूतsuvīryamādityajūta एधतेedhate |

सदाsadā रायाrāyā पुरुस्प्र्हाpuruspṛhā ||
Kine, steeds, and hero strength he gains, and prospers, by the Ādityas sped,
Ever in wealth which all desire.
8.46.5
तमिन्द्रंtamindraṃ दानमीमहेdānamīmahe शवसानमभीर्वमśavasānamabhīrvam |

ईशानंīśānaṃ रायrāya ईमहेīmahe ||
We pray to Indra for his gift, to him the Fearless and the Strong,
We pray to him the Lord of wealth.
8.46.6
तस्मिनtasmin हिhi सन्त्यूतयोsantyūtayo विश्वाviśvā अभीरवःabhīravaḥ सचाsacā |

तमाtamā वहन्तुvahantu सप्तयःsaptayaḥ पुरूवसुंpurūvasuṃ मदायmadāya हरयःharayaḥ सुतमsutam ||
For verily combined in him are all the fearless powers of aid.
Him, rich in wealth, let swift Steeds bring to us, his Bays, to Soma juice for his carouse:
8.46.7
यस्तेyaste मदोmado वरेण्योvareṇyo यya इन्द्रindra वर्त्रहन्तमःvṛtrahantamaḥ |

यya आददिःādadiḥ सवर्न्र्भिर्यःsvarnṛbhiryaḥ पर्तनासुpṛtanāsu दुष्टरःduṣṭaraḥ ||
Yea, that most excellent carouse, Indra, which slays most enemies,
With Heroes wins the light of heaven, and is invincible in war:
8.46.8
योyo दुष्टरोduṣṭaro विश्ववारviśvavāra शरवाय्योśravāyyo वाजेष्वस्तिvājeṣvasti तरुताtarutā |

सनःsanaḥ शविष्ठśaviṣṭha सवनाsavanā वसोvaso गहिghahi गमेमghamema गोमतिghomati वरजेvraje ||
Which merits fame, all-bountiful! and, unsubdued, hath victory in deeds of might.
So come to our libations, Strongest! Excellent! May we obtain a stall of kine.
8.46.9
गव्योghavyo षुṣu णोṇo यथाyathā पुराश्वयोतpurāśvayota रथयाrathayā |

वरिवस्यvarivasya महामहmahāmaha ||
Responding to our wish for cows, for steeds, and chariots, as of old,
Be gracious, Greatest of the Great
8.46.10
नहिnahi तेte शूरśūra राधसो.अन्तंrādhaso.antaṃ विन्दामिvindāmi सत्राsatrā |

दशस्याdaśasyā नोno मघवनmaghavan नूnū चिदद्रिवोcidadrivo धियोdhiyo वाजेभिराविथvājebhirāvitha ||
For, Hero, nowhere can I find the bounds of thy munificence.
Still do thou favour us, O Bolt-armed Maghavan: with strength hast thou rewarded hymns.
8.46.11
यya रष्वःṛṣvaḥ शरावयत्सखाśrāvayatsakhā विश्वेतviśvet सsa वेदveda जनिमाjanimā पुरुष्टुतःpuruṣṭutaḥ |

तंtaṃ विश्वेviśve मानुषाmānuṣā युगेन्द्रंyughendraṃ हवन्तेhavante तविषंtaviṣaṃ यतस्रुचःyatasrucaḥ ||
High, glorifier of his friend, he knows all generations, he whom many praise.
All races of mankind with ladies lifted up invoke that Mighty Indra's aid.
8.46.12
सsa नोno वाजेष्वविताvājeṣvavitā पुरूवसुःpurūvasuḥ पुरःस्थाताpuraḥsthātā मघवाmaghavā वर्त्रहाvṛtrahā भुवतbhuvat ||
Be he our Champion and Protector in great deeds, rich in all wealth, the Vṛtra-slayer, Maghavan.
8.46.13
अभिabhi वोvo वीरमन्धसोvīramandhaso मदेषुmadeṣu गायghāya गिराghirā महाmahā विचेतसमvicetasam |

इन्द्रंindraṃ नामnāma शरुत्यंśrutyaṃ शाकिनंśākinaṃ वचोvaco यथाyathā ||
In the wild raptures of the juice sing to your Hero with high laud, to him the Wise,
To Indra, glorious in his name, the Mighty One, even as the hymn alloweth it.
8.46.14
ददीdadī रेक्णस्तन्वेrekṇastanve ददिर्वसुdadirvasu ददिर्वाजेषुdadirvājeṣu पुरुहूतpuruhūta वाजिनमvājinam |

नूनमथnūnamatha ||
Thou givest wealth to me myself, thou givest treasure, Excellent! and the strong steed,
O Much-invoked, in deeds of might, yea, even now.
8.46.15
विश्वेषामिरज्यन्तंviśveṣāmirajyantaṃ वसूनांvasūnāṃ सासह्वांसंsāsahvāṃsaṃ चिदस्यcidasya वर्पसःvarpasaḥ |

कर्पयतोkṛpayato नूनमत्यथnūnamatyatha ||
Him, Sovran Ruler of all precious things, who even hath power o’er this fair form of his,
As now it taketh shape, and afterward,
8.46.16
महःmahaḥ सुsu वोvo अरमिषेaramiṣe सतवामहेstavāmahe मीळ्हुषेmīḷhuṣe अरंगमायaraṃghamāya जग्मयेjaghmaye |

यज्ञेभिर्गीर्भिर्विश्वमनुषांyajñebhirghīrbhirviśvamanuṣāṃ मरुतामियक्षसिmarutāmiyakṣasi गायेत्वाghāyetvā नमसाnamasā गिराghirā ||
We praise, so that the Mighty One may speed to you, Pourer of
bounties
, Traveller, prepared to go.
Thou favourest the Maruts known to all, by song and sacrifice.
With song and praise I sing to thee.
8.46.17
येye पातयन्तेpātayante अज्मभिर्गिरीणांajmabhirghirīṇāṃ सनुभिरेषामsnubhireṣām |

यज्ञंyajñaṃ महिष्वणीनांmahiṣvaṇīnāṃ सुम्नंsumnaṃ तुविष्वणीनांtuviṣvaṇīnāṃ पराध्वरेprādhvare ||
We in the sacrifice perform their will whose voice is lifted high,
The worship of those Thundering Ones who o’er the ridges of these mountains fly in troops.
8.46.18
परभङगंprabhaṅghaṃ दुर्मतीनामिन्द्रdurmatīnāmindra शविष्ठाśaviṣṭhā भरbhara |

रयिमस्मभ्यंrayimasmabhyaṃ युज्यंyujyaṃ चोदयन्मतेcodayanmate जयेष्ठंjyeṣṭhaṃ चोदयन्मतेcodayanmate ||
O Indra, Mightiest, bring us that which crushes men of evil minds,
Wealth suited to our needs, O Stirrer of the thought, best wealth, O thou who stirrest thought.
8.46.19
सनितःsanitaḥ सुसनितरुग्रsusanitarughra चित्रcitra चेतिष्ठcetiṣṭha सून्र्तsūnṛta |

परासहाprāsahā सम्राटsamrāṭ सहुरिंsahuriṃ सहन्तंsahantaṃ भुज्युंbhujyuṃ वाजेषुvājeṣu पूर्व्यमpūrvyam ||
O Winner, noble winner, strong, wondrous, most splendid, excellent,
Sole Lord of victory, bring all-overpowering wealth, joy-giving, chief in deeds of might.
8.46.20
आā सsa एतुetu यya ईवदानदेवःīvadānadevaḥ पूर्तमाददेpūrtamādade |

यथाyathā चिदcid वशोvaśo अश्व्यःaśvyaḥ पर्थुश्रवसिpṛthuśravasi कानीते.अस्याkānīte.asyā वयुष्याददेvyuṣyādade ||
Now let the godless man approach who hath received reward so great
As Vaśa, Aśvya, when this light of morning dawned, received from Pṛthuśravas, from Kanīta's son.
8.46.21
षष्टिंṣaṣṭiṃ सहस्राश्व्यस्यायुतासनमुष्ट्रानांsahasrāśvyasyāyutāsanamuṣṭrānāṃ विंशतिंशताviṃśatiṃśatā |

दशdaśa शयावीनांśyāvīnāṃ शताśatā दशdaśa तर्यरुषीणांtryaruṣīṇāṃ दशdaśa गवांghavāṃ सहस्राsahasrā ||
Steeds sixty thousand and ten thousand kine, and twenty hundred camels I obtained;
Ten hundred brown in hue, and other ten red in three spots: in all, ten thousand kine.
8.46.22
दशdaśa शयावाśyāvā रधद्रयोṛdhadrayo वीतवारासvītavārāsa आशवःāśavaḥ |

मथ्राmathrā नेमिंnemiṃ निni वाव्र्तुःvāvṛtuḥ ||
Ten browns that make my wealth increase, fleet steeds whose tails are long and fair,
Turn with swift whirl my chariot wheel;
8.46.23
दानासःdānāsaḥ पर्थुश्रवसःpṛthuśravasaḥ कानीतस्यkānītasya सुराधसःsurādhasaḥ |

रथंrathaṃ हिरण्ययंhiraṇyayaṃ ददनdadan मंहिष्टःmaṃhiṣṭaḥ सूरिरभूदsūrirabhūd वर्षिष्ठमक्र्तvarṣiṣṭhamakṛta शरवःśravaḥ ||
The gifts which Pṛthuśravas gave, Kanīta's son munificent.
He gave a chariot wrought of gold: the prince was passing bountiful, and won himself most lofty fame.
8.46.24
आā नोno वायोvāyo महेmahe तनेtane याहिyāhi मखायmakhāya पाजसेpājase |

वयंvayaṃ हिhi तेte चक्र्माcakṛmā भूरिbhūri दावनेdāvane सद्यश्चिनsadyaścin महिmahi दावनेdāvane ||
Come thou to this great rite of ours, Vāyu! to give us vigorous light.
We have served thee that thou mightest give much to us, yea, mightest quickly give great wealth.
8.46.25
योyo अश्वेभिर्वहतेaśvebhirvahate वस्तvasta उस्रास्त्रिःusrāstriḥ सप्तsapta सप्ततीनामsaptatīnām |

एभिःebhiḥ सोमेभिःsomebhiḥ सोमसुद्भिःsomasudbhiḥ सोमपाsomapā दानायdānāya शुक्रपूतपाःśukrapūtapāḥ ||
Who with thrice seven times seventy horses comes to us, invested with the rays of morn,
Through these our Soma-draughts and those who press, to give, drinker of pure bright Soma Juice.
8.46.26
योyo मma इमंimaṃ चिदुcidu तमनामन्दच्चित्रंtmanāmandaccitraṃ दावनेdāvane |

अरट्वेaraṭve अक्षेनहुषेakṣenahuṣe सुक्र्त्वनिsukṛtvani सुक्र्त्तरायsukṛttarāya सुक्रतुःsukratuḥ ||
Who hath inclined this glorious one, bounteous himself, to give me gifts.
Borne on firm chariot with the prosperous Nahuṣa, wise, to a man yet more devout.
8.46.27
उचथ्येucathye वपुषिvapuṣi यःyaḥ सवराळsvarāḷ उतuta वायोvāyo घर्तस्नाःghṛtasnāḥ |

अश्वेषितंaśveṣitaṃ रजेषितंrajeṣitaṃ शुनेषितंśuneṣitaṃ पराज्मprājma तदिदंtadidaṃ नुnu ततtat ||
Sole Lord in beauty meet for praise, O Vāyu, dropping fatness down,
Hurried along by steeds, by camels, and by hounds, spreads forth thy train: even this it is.
8.46.28
अधadha परियमिषिरायpriyamiṣirāya षस्टिंṣasṭiṃ सहस्रासनमsahasrāsanam |

अश्वानामिनaśvānāmin नna वर्ष्णामvṛṣṇām ||
So, as a prize dear to the strong, the sixty thousand have I gained,
Bulls that resemble vigorous steeds.
8.46.29
गावोghāvo नna यूथमुपyūthamupa यन्तिyanti वध्रयvadhraya उपupa माmā यन्तिyanti वध्रयःvadhrayaḥ ||
To me come oxen like a herd, yea, unto me the oxen come.
8.46.30
अधadha यच्चारथेyaccārathe गणेghaṇe शतमुष्ट्रानचिक्रदतśatamuṣṭrānacikradat |

अधadha शवित्नेषुśvitneṣu विंशतिंviṃśatiṃ शताśatā ||
And in the grazing herd he made a hundred camels bleat for me,
And twenty hundred mid the white.
8.46.31
शतंśataṃ दासेdāse बल्बूथेbalbūthe विप्रस्तरुक्षviprastarukṣa आā ददेdade |

तेte तेte वायविमेvāyavime जनाjanā मदन्तीन्द्रगोपाmadantīndraghopā मदन्तिmadanti देवगोपाःdevaghopāḥ ||
A hundred has the sage received, Dāsa Balbūtha's and Tarukṣa's gifts.
These are thy people, Vāyu, who rejoice with Indra for their guard, rejoice with Gods for guards.
8.46.32
अधadha सयाsyā योषणाyoṣaṇā महीmahī परतीचीpratīcī वशमश्व्यमvaśamaśvyam |

अधिरुक्माadhirukmā विvi नीयतेnīyate ||
And now to Vaśa Aśvya here this stately woman is led forth,
Adorned with ornaments of gold.
8.46.33
