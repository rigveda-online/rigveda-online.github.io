HYMN XLIII. INDRA.

आā याह्यर्वांyāhyarvāṃ उपupa वन्धुरेष्ठास्तवेदनुvandhureṣṭhāstavedanu परदिवःpradivaḥ सोमपेयमsomapeyam |

परियाpriyā सखायाsakhāyā विvi मुचोपmucopa बर्हिस्त्वामिमेbarhistvāmime हव्यवाहोhavyavāho हवन्तेhavante ||
MOUNTED upon thy chariot-seat approach us: thine is the Soma-draught from days aforetime.
Loose for the sacred grass thy dear companions. These men who bring oblation call thee hither.
3.43.1
आā याहिyāhi पूर्वीरतिpūrvīrati चर्षणीरानर्यcarṣaṇīrānarya आशिषāśiṣa उपupa नोno हरिभ्यामharibhyām |

इमाimā हिhi तवाtvā मतयmataya सतोमतष्टाstomataṣṭā इन्द्रindra हवन्तेhavante सख्यंsakhyaṃ जुषाणाःjuṣāṇāḥ ||
Come our true Friend, passing by many people; come with thy two Bay Steeds to our devotions;
For these our hymns are calling thee, O Indra, hymns formed for praise, soliciting thy friendship.
3.43.2
आā नोno यज्ञंyajñaṃ नमोव्र्धंnamovṛdhaṃ सजोषाsajoṣā इन्द्रindra देवdeva हरिभिर्याहिharibhiryāhi तूयमtūyam |

अहंahaṃ हिhi तवाtvā मतिभिर्जोहवीमिmatibhirjohavīmi घर्तप्रयाःghṛtaprayāḥ सधमादेsadhamāde मधूनामmadhūnām ||
Pleased, with thy Bay Steeds, Indra, God, come quickly to this our sacrifice that heightens worship;
For with my thoughts, presenting oil to feed thee, I call thee to the feast of sweet libations.
3.43.3
आā चca तवामेताtvāmetā वर्षणाvṛṣaṇā वहातोvahāto हरीharī सखायाsakhāyā सुधुराsudhurā सवङगाsvaṅghā |

धानावदिन्द्रःdhānāvadindraḥ सवनंsavanaṃ जुषाणःjuṣāṇaḥ सखाsakhā सख्युःsakhyuḥ शर्णवदśṛṇavad वन्दनानिvandanāni ||
Yea, let thy two Bay Stallions bear thee hither, well limbed and good to draw, thy dear companions.
Pleased with the corn-blent offering which we bring thee, may Indra, Friend, hear his friend's adoration.
3.43.4
कुविनkuvin माmā गोपांghopāṃ करसेkarase जनस्यjanasya कुविदkuvid राजानंrājānaṃ मघवन्न्र्जीषिनmaghavannṛjīṣin |

कुविनkuvin मma रषिंṛṣiṃ पपिवांसंpapivāṃsaṃ सुतस्यsutasya कुविनkuvin मेme वस्वोvasvo अम्र्तस्यamṛtasya शिक्षाःśikṣāḥ ||
Wilt thou not make me guardian of the people, make me, impetuous Maghavan, their ruler?
Make me a Ṛṣi having drunk of Soma? Wilt thou not give me wealth that lasts for ever?
3.43.5
आā तवाtvā बर्हन्तोbṛhanto हरयोharayo युजानाyujānā अर्वागिन्द्रarvāghindra सधमादोsadhamādo वहन्तुvahantu |

परpra येye दविताdvitā दिवdiva रञ्जन्त्याताःṛñjantyātāḥ सुसम्म्र्ष्टासोsusammṛṣṭāso वर्षभस्यvṛṣabhasya मूराःmūrāḥ ||
Yoked to thy chariot, led thy tall Bays, Indra, companions of thy banquet, bear thee hither,
Who from of old press to heaven's farthest limits, the Bull's impetuous and well-groomed Horses.
3.43.6
इन्द्रindra पिबpiba वर्षधूतस्यvṛṣadhūtasya वर्ष्णvṛṣṇa आā यंyaṃ तेte शयेनśyena उशतेuśate जभारjabhāra |

यस्यyasya मदेmade चयावयसिcyāvayasi परpra कर्ष्टीर्यस्यkṛṣṭīryasya मदेmade अपapa गोत्राghotrā ववर्थvavartha ||
Drink of the strong pressed out by strong ones, Indra, that which the Falcon brought thee when thou longedst;
In whose wild joy thou stirrest up the people, in whose wild joy thou didst unbar the cow-stalls.
3.43.7
शुनंśunaṃ हुवेमhuvema ...... ||
Call we on Indra, Makhavan, auspicious, best Hero in the fight where spoil is gathered;
The Strong, who listens, who gives aid in battles, who slays the Vṛtras, wins and gathers riches.
3.43.8
