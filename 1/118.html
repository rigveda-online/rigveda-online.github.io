HYMN CXVIII. AŚVINS.

आā वांvāṃ रथोratho अश्विनाaśvinā शयेनपत्वाśyenapatvā सुम्र्ळीकःsumṛḷīkaḥ सववानsvavān यात्वर्वांyātvarvāṃ |

योyo मर्त्यस्यmartyasya मनसोmanaso जवीयानjavīyān तरिवन्धुरोtrivandhuro वर्षणाvṛṣaṇā वातरंहाःvātaraṃhāḥ ||
FLYING, with falcons, may your chariot, Aśvins, most gracious, bringing friendly
help, come hither,—
Your chariot, swifter than the mind of mortal, fleet as the wind, three-seated O ye Mighty.
1.118.1
तरिवन्धुरेणtrivandhureṇa तरिव्र्ताtrivṛtā रथेनrathena तरिचक्रेणtricakreṇa सुव्र्ताsuvṛtā यातमर्वाकyātamarvāk |

पिन्वतंpinvataṃ गाghā जिन्वतमर्वतोjinvatamarvato नोno वर्धयतमश्विनाvardhayatamaśvinā वीरमस्मेvīramasme ||
Come to us with your chariot triple seated, three-wheeled, of triple form, that rolleth lightly.
Fill full our cows, give mettle to our horses, and make each hero son grow strong, O Aśvins.
1.118.2
परवद्यामनाpravadyāmanā सुव्र्ताsuvṛtā रथेनrathena दस्राविमंdasrāvimaṃ शर्णुतंśṛṇutaṃ शलोकमद्रेःślokamadreḥ |

किमङगkimaṅgha वांvāṃ परत्यवर्तिंpratyavartiṃ गमिष्ठाहुर्विप्रासोश्विनाghamiṣṭhāhurviprāsoaśvinā पुराजाःpurājāḥ ||
With your well-rolling car, descending swiftly, hear this the press-stone's song, ye Wonder-Workers.
How then have ancient sages said, O Aśvins, that ye most swiftly come to stay affliction?
1.118.3
आā वांvāṃ शयेनासोśyenāso अश्विनाaśvinā वहन्तुvahantu रथेrathe युक्तासyuktāsa आशवःāśavaḥ पतंगाःpataṃghāḥ |

येye अप्तुरोapturo दिव्यासोdivyāso नna गर्ध्राghṛdhrā अभिabhi परयोprayo नासत्याnāsatyā वहन्तिvahanti ||
O Aśvins, let your falcons bear you hither, yoked to your chariot, swift, with flying pinions,
Which, ever active, like the airy eagles, carry you, O Nāsatyas, to the banquet.
1.118.4
आā वांvāṃ रथंrathaṃ युवतिस्तिष्ठदत्रyuvatistiṣṭhadatra जुष्ट्वीjuṣṭvī नराnarā दुहितासूर्यस्यduhitāsūryasya |

परिpari वामश्वाvāmaśvā वपुषःvapuṣaḥ पतंगाpataṃghā वयोvayo वहन्त्वरुषाvahantvaruṣā अभीकेabhīke ||
The youthful Daughter of the Sun, delighting in you, ascended there your chariot, Heroes.
Borne on their swift wings let your beauteous horses, your birds of ruddy hue, convey you near us.
1.118.5
उदud वन्दनमैरतंvandanamairataṃ दंसनाभिरुदdaṃsanābhirud रेभंrebhaṃ दस्राdasrā वर्षणाvṛṣaṇā शचीभिःśacībhiḥ |

निषniṣ टौग्र्यंṭaughryaṃ पारयथःpārayathaḥ समुद्रातsamudrāt पुनश्च्यवानंpunaścyavānaṃ चक्रथुर्युवानमcakrathuryuvānam ||
Ye raised up Vandana, strong Wonder-Workers! with great might, and with power ye rescued Rebha.
From out the sea ye saved the son of Tugra, and gave his youth again unto Cyavāna.
1.118.6
युवमत्रये.अवनीतायyuvamatraye.avanītāya तप्तमूर्जमोमानमश्विनावधत्तमtaptamūrjamomānamaśvināvadhattam |

युवंyuvaṃ कण्वायापिरिप्तायkaṇvāyāpiriptāya चक्षुःcakṣuḥ परत्यधत्तंpratyadhattaṃ सुष्टुतिंsuṣṭutiṃ जुजुषाणाjujuṣāṇā ||
To Atri, cast down to the fire that scorched him, ye gave, O Aśvins, strengthening food and favour.
Accepting his fair praises with approval, ye gave his eyes again to blinded Kaṇva.
1.118.7
युवंyuvaṃ धेनुंdhenuṃ शयवेśayave नाधितायापिन्वतमश्विनाnādhitāyāpinvatamaśvinā पूर्व्यायpūrvyāya |

अमुञ्चतंamuñcataṃ वर्तिकामंहसोvartikāmaṃhaso निःniḥ परतिprati जङघांjaṅghāṃ विश्पलायाviśpalāyā अधत्तमadhattam ||
For ancient Śayu in his sore affliction ye caused his cow to swell with milk, O Aśvins.
The quail from her great misery ye delivered, and a new leg for Viśpalā provided.
1.118.8
युवंyuvaṃ शवेतंśvetaṃ पेदवpedava इन्द्रजूतमहिहनमश्विनादत्तमश्वमindrajūtamahihanamaśvinādattamaśvam |

जोहूत्रमर्योjohūtramaryo अभिभूतिमुग्रंabhibhūtimughraṃ सहस्रसांsahasrasāṃ वर्षणंvṛṣaṇaṃ वीड्वङगमvīḍvaṅgham ||
A white horse, Aśvins, ye bestowed on Pedu, a serpent-slaying steed sent down by Indra,
Loud-neighing, conquering the foe, high-mettled, firm-limbed and vigorous, winning thousand treasures.
1.118.9
ताtā वांvāṃ नराnarā सववसेsvavase सुजाताsujātā हवामहेhavāmahe अश्विनाaśvinā नाधमानाःnādhamānāḥ |

आā नna उपupa वसुमताvasumatā रथेनrathena गिरोghiro जुसानाjusānā सुवितायsuvitāya यातमyātam ||
Such as ye are, O nobly born, O Heroes, we in our trouble call on you for succour.
Accepting these our songs, for our wellbeing come to us on your chariot treasure-laden.
1.118.10
आā शयेनस्यśyenasya जवसाjavasā नूतनेनास्मेnūtanenāsme यातंyātaṃ नासत्याnāsatyā सजोषाःsajoṣāḥ |

हवेhave हिhi वामश्विनाvāmaśvinā रातहव्यःrātahavyaḥ शश्वत्तमायाśaśvattamāyā उषसोuṣaso वयुष्टौvyuṣṭau ||
Come unto us combined in love, Nāsatyas come with the fresh swift vigour of the falcon.
Bearing oblations I invoke you, Aśvins, at the first break of everlasting morning.
1.118.11
