HYMN CX. ṚBHUS.

ततंtataṃ मेme अपस्तदुapastadu तायतेtāyate पुनःpunaḥ सवादिष्ठाsvādiṣṭhā धीतिरुचथायdhītirucathāya शस्यतेśasyate |

अयंayaṃ समुद्रsamudra इहiha विश्वदेव्यःviśvadevyaḥ सवाहाक्र्तस्यsvāhākṛtasya समुsamu तर्प्णुतtṛpṇuta रभवःṛbhavaḥ ||
THE holy work I wrought before is wrought again: my sweetest hymn is sung to celebrate your praise.
Here, O ye Ṛbhus, is this sea for all the Gods: sate you with Soma offered with the hallowing word.
1.110.1
आभोगयंābhoghayaṃ परpra यदिछन्तyadichanta ऐतनापाकाःaitanāpākāḥ पराञ्चोprāñco ममmama केke चिदापयःcidāpayaḥ |

सौधन्वनासश्चरितस्यsaudhanvanāsaścaritasya भूमनागछतbhūmanāghachata सवितुर्दाशुषोsaviturdāśuṣo गर्हमghṛham ||
When, seeking your enjoyment onward from afar, ye, certain of my kinsmen, wandered on your way,
Sons of Sudhanvan, after your long journeying, ye came unto the home of liberal Savitar.
1.110.2
ततtat सविताsavitā वो.अम्र्तत्वामासुवदगोह्यंvo.amṛtatvāmāsuvadaghohyaṃ यच्छ्रवयन्तyacchravayanta ऐतनaitana |

तयंtyaṃ चिच्चमसमसुरस्यciccamasamasurasya भक्षणमेकंbhakṣaṇamekaṃ सन्तमक्र्णुताsantamakṛṇutā चतुर्वयमcaturvayam ||
Savitar therefore gave you immortality, because ye came proclaiming him whom naught can hide;
And this the drinking-chalice of the Asura, which till that time was one, ye made to be fourfold.
1.110.3
विष्ट्वीviṣṭvī शमीśamī तरणित्वेनtaraṇitvena वाघतोvāghato मर्तासःmartāsaḥ सन्तोsanto अम्र्तत्वमानशुःamṛtatvamānaśuḥ |

सौधन्वनाsaudhanvanā रभवःṛbhavaḥ सूरचक्षसःsūracakṣasaḥ संवत्सरेsaṃvatsare समप्र्च्यन्तsamapṛcyanta धीतिभिःdhītibhiḥ ||
When they had served with zeal at sacrifice as priests, they, mortal as they were, gained immortality.
The Ṛbhus, children of Sudhanvan, bright as suns, were in a year's course made associate with prayers.
1.110.4
कषेत्रमिवkṣetramiva विvi ममुस्तेजनेनमेकंmamustejanenamekaṃ पात्रंpātraṃ रभवोṛbhavo जेहमानमjehamānam |

उपस्तुताupastutā उपमंupamaṃ नाधमानाnādhamānā अमर्त्येषुamartyeṣu शरवśrava इछमानाःichamānāḥ ||
The Ṛbhus, with a rod measured, as ’twere a field, the single sacrificial chalice. wide of mouth,
Lauded of all who saw, praying for what is best, desiring glorious fame among Immortal Gods.
1.110.5
आā मनीषामन्तरिक्षस्यmanīṣāmantarikṣasya नर्भ्यःnṛbhyaḥ सरुचेवsruceva घर्तंghṛtaṃ जुहवामjuhavāma विद्मनाvidmanā |

तरणित्वाtaraṇitvā येye पितुरस्यpiturasya सश्चिरsaścira रभवोṛbhavo वाजमरुहनvājamaruhan दिवोdivo रजःrajaḥ ||
As oil in ladles, we through knowledge will present unto the Heroes of the firmament our hymn,—
The Ṛbhus who came near with this great Father's speed, and rose to heaven's high sphere to eat the strengthening food.
1.110.6
रभुर्नṛbhurna इन्द्रःindraḥ शवसाśavasā नवीयानnavīyān रभुर्वाजेभिर्वसुभिर्वसुर्ददिःṛbhurvājebhirvasubhirvasurdadiḥ |

युष्माकंyuṣmākaṃ देवाdevā अवसाहनिavasāhani परिये.अभिpriye.abhi तिष्ठेमप्र्त्सुतीरसुन्वतामtiṣṭhemapṛtsutīrasunvatām ||
Ṛbhu to us is Indra freshest in his might, Ṛbhu with powers and wealth is giver of rich gifts.
Gods, through your favour may we on the happy day quell the attacks of those who pour no offerings forth.
1.110.7
निश्चर्मणniścarmaṇa रभवोṛbhavo गामपिंशतghāmapiṃśata संsaṃ वत्सेनास्र्जताvatsenāsṛjatā मातरंmātaraṃ पुनःpunaḥ |

सौधन्वनासःsaudhanvanāsaḥ सवपस्ययाsvapasyayā नरोnaro जिव्रीjivrī युवानाyuvānā पितराक्र्णोतनpitarākṛṇotana ||
Out of a skin, O Ṛbhus, once ye formed a cow, and brought the mother close unto her calf again.
Sons of Sudhanvan, Heroes, with surpassing skill ye made your aged Parents youthful as before.
1.110.8
वाजेभिर्नोvājebhirno वाजसातावविड्ढ्यvājasātāvaviḍḍhy रभुमानिन्द्रṛbhumānindra चित्रमाcitramā दर्षिdarṣi राधःrādhaḥ |

तनtan नोno ...... ||
Help us with strength where spoil is won, O Indra: joined with the Ṛbhus give us varied bounty.
This prayer of ours may Varuṇa grant, and Mitra, and Aditi and Sindhu, Earth and Heaven.
1.110.9
