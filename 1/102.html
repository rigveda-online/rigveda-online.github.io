HYMN CII. INDRA.

इमांimāṃ तेte धियंdhiyaṃ परpra भरेbhare महोmaho महीमस्यmahīmasya सतोत्रेstotre धिषणायतdhiṣaṇāyat तta आनजेānaje |

तमुत्सवेtamutsave चca परसवेprasave चca सासहिमिन्द्रंsāsahimindraṃ देवासःdevāsaḥ शवसामदन्ननुśavasāmadannanu ||
To thee the Mighty One I bring this mighty hymn, for thy desire hath been gratified by my laud.
In Indra, yea in him victorious through his strength, the Gods have joyed at feast and when the Soma flowed.
1.102.1
अस्यasya शरवोśravo नद्यःnadyaḥ सप्तsapta बिभ्रतिbibhrati दयावाक्षामाdyāvākṣāmā पर्थिवीpṛthivī दर्शतंdarśataṃ वपुःvapuḥ |

अस्मेasme सूर्याचन्द्रमसाभिचक्षेsūryācandramasābhicakṣe शरद्धेśraddhe कमिन्द्रkamindra चरतोcarato वितर्तुरमvitarturam ||
The Seven Rivers bear his glory far and wide, and heaven and sky and earth display his comely form.
The Sun and Moon in change alternate run their course, that we, O Indra, may behold and may have faith.
1.102.2
तंtaṃ समाsmā रथंrathaṃ मघवन्न्प्रावmaghavannprāva सातयेsātaye जैत्रंjaitraṃ यंyaṃ तेte अनुमदामanumadāma संगमेsaṃghame |

आजाājā नna इन्द्रindra मनसाmanasā पुरुष्टुतpuruṣṭuta तवायद्भ्योtvāyadbhyo मघवञ्छर्मmaghavañcharma यछyacha नःnaḥ ||
Maghavan, grant us that same car to bring us spoil, thy conquering car in which we joy in shock of fight.
Thou, Indra, whom our hearts praise highly in the war, grant shelter, Maghavan, to us who love thee well.
1.102.3
वयंvayaṃ जयेमjayema तवयाtvayā युजाyujā वर्तमस्माकमंशमुदवाvṛtamasmākamaṃśamudavā भरे-भरेbhare-bhare |

अस्मभ्यमिन्द्रasmabhyamindra वरिवःvarivaḥ सुगंsughaṃ कर्धिkṛdhi परpra शत्रूणांमघवनśatrūṇāṃmaghavan वर्ष्ण्याvṛṣṇyā रुजruja ||
Encourage thou our side in every fight: may we, with thee for our ally, conquer the foeman's host.
Indra, bestow on us joy and felicity break down, O Maghavan, the vigour of our foes.
1.102.4
नानाnānā हिhi तवाtvā हवमानाhavamānā जनाjanā इमेime धनानांdhanānāṃ धर्तरवसाविपन्यवःdhartaravasāvipanyavaḥ |

अस्माकंasmākaṃ समाsmā रथमाrathamā तिष्ठtiṣṭha सातयेsātaye जैत्रंहीन्द्रjaitraṃhīndra निभ्र्तंnibhṛtaṃ मनस्तवmanastava ||
For here in divers ways these men invoking thee, holder of treasures, sing hymns to win thine aid.
Ascend the car that thou mayest bring spoil to us, for, Indra, thy fixt winneth the victory.
1.102.5
गोजिताghojitā बाहूbāhū अमितक्रतुःamitakratuḥ सिमःsimaḥ कर्मनkarman कर्मञ्छतमूतिःkarmañchatamūtiḥ खजंकरःkhajaṃkaraḥ |

अकल्पakalpa इन्द्रःindraḥ परतिमानमोजसाथाpratimānamojasāthā जनाjanā विह्वयन्तेvihvayante सिषासवःsiṣāsavaḥ ||
His arms win kine, his power is boundless in each act best, with a hundred helps, waker of battle's din
Is Indra: none may rival him in mighty strength. Hence, eager for the spoil the people call on him.
1.102.6
उतut तेte शतानśatān मघवन्नुच्चmaghavannucca भूयसbhūyasa उतut सहस्रादsahasrād रिरिचेririce कर्ष्टिषुkṛṣṭiṣu शरवःśravaḥ |

अमात्रंamātraṃ तवाtvā धिषणाdhiṣaṇā तित्विषेtitviṣe मह्यधाmahyadhā वर्त्राणिvṛtrāṇi जिघ्नसेjighnase पुरन्दरpurandara ||
Thy glory, Maghavan, exceeds a hundred yea, more than a hundred, than a thousand mid the folk,
The great bowl hath inspirited thee boundlessly: so mayst thou slay the Vṛtras, breaker-down of forts!
1.102.7
तरिविष्टिधातुtriviṣṭidhātu परतिमानमोजसस्तिस्रोpratimānamojasastisro भूमीर्न्र्पतेbhūmīrnṛpate तरीणिtrīṇi रोचनाrocanā |

अतीदंatīdaṃ विश्वंviśvaṃ भुवनंbhuvanaṃ ववक्षिथाशत्रुरिन्द्रजनुषाvavakṣithāśatrurindrajanuṣā सनादसिsanādasi ||
Of thy great might there is a three counterpart, the three earths, Lord men and the three realms of light.
Above this whole world, Indra, thou hast waxen great: without a foe art thou, nature, from of old.
1.102.8
तवांtvāṃ देवेषुdeveṣu परथमंprathamaṃ हवामहेhavāmahe तवंtvaṃ बभूथbabhūtha पर्तनासुpṛtanāsu सासहिःsāsahiḥ |

सेमंsemaṃ नःnaḥ कारुमुपमन्युमुद्भिदमिन्द्रःkārumupamanyumudbhidamindraḥ कर्णोतुkṛṇotu परसवेprasave रथंrathaṃ पुरःpuraḥ ||
We invocate thee first among the Deities: thou hast become a mighty Conquer in fight.
May Indra fill with spirit this our singer's heart, and make our car impetuous, foremost in attack.
1.102.9
तवंtvaṃ जिगेथjighetha नna धनाdhanā रुरोधिथार्भेष्वाजाrurodhithārbheṣvājā मघवनmaghavan महत्सुmahatsu चca |

तवामुग्रमवसेtvāmughramavase संsaṃ शिशीमस्यथाśiśīmasyathā नna इन्द्रindra हवनेषुhavaneṣu चोदयcodaya ||
Thou hast prevailed, and hast not kept the booty back, in trifling battles in those of great account.
We make thee keen, the Mighty One, succour us: inspire us, Maghavan, when we defy the foe.
1.102.10
विश्वाहेन्द्रोviśvāhendro ...... ||
May Indra evermore be our Protector, and unimperilled may we win the booty.
This prayer of ours may Varuṇa grant and Mitra, and Aditi and Sindhu, Earth and Heaven.
