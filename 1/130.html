HYMN CXXX. INDRA.

एन्द्रendra याह्युपyāhyupa नःnaḥ परावतोparāvato नायमछाnāyamachā विदथानीवvidathānīva सत्पतिरस्तंsatpatirastaṃ राजेवrājeva सत्पतिःsatpatiḥ | हवामहेhavāmahe तवाtvā वयंvayaṃ परयस्वन्तःprayasvantaḥ सुतेsute सचाsacā |

पुत्रासोputrāso नna पितरंpitaraṃ वाजसातयेvājasātaye मंहिष्ठंmaṃhiṣṭhaṃ वाजसातयेvājasātaye ||
Come to us, Indra, from afar, conducting us even as a lord of heroes to the gatherings, home, like a King, his heroes' lord.
We come with gifts of pleasant food, with juice poured forth, invoking thee,
As sons invite a sire, that thou mayst get thee strength thee, bounteousest, to get thee strength.
1.130.1
पिबाpibā सोममिन्द्रsomamindra सुवानमद्रिभिःsuvānamadribhiḥ कोशेनkośena सिक्तमवतंsiktamavataṃ नवंसगस्तात्र्षाणोnavaṃsaghastātṛṣāṇo नna वंसगःvaṃsaghaḥ | मदायmadāya हर्यतयharyataya तेte तुविष्टमायtuviṣṭamāya धायसेdhāyase |

आā तवाtvā यछन्तुyachantu हरितोharito नna सूर्यमहाविश्वेवsūryamahāviśveva सूर्यमsūryam ||
O Indra, drink the Soma juice pressed out with stones. poured from the reservoir, as an ox drinks the spring, a very thirsty bull the spring.
For the sweet draught that gladdens thee, for mightiest freshening of thy strength.
Let thy Bay Horses bring thee hither as the Sun, as every day they bring the Sun.
1.130.2
अविन्ददavindad दिवोdivo निहितंnihitaṃ गुहाghuhā निधिंnidhiṃ वेर्नverna गर्भंgharbhaṃ परिवीतमश्मन्यनन्तेparivītamaśmanyanante अन्तरश्मनिantaraśmani | वरजंvrajaṃ वज्रिvajri गवामिवghavāmiva सिषासन्नङगिरस्तमःsiṣāsannaṅghirastamaḥ |

अपाव्र्णोदिषapāvṛṇodiṣa इन्द्रःindraḥ परीव्र्ताparīvṛtā दवारdvāra इषःiṣaḥ परीव्र्ताःparīvṛtāḥ ||
He found the treasure brought from heaven that lay concealed, close-hidden, like the nestling of a bird, in rock, enclosed in never-ending rock.
Best Aṅgiras, bolt-armed, he strove to win, as ’twere, the stall of kine;
So Indra hath disclosed the food concealed, disclosed the doors, the food that lay concealed.
1.130.3
दाद्र्हाणोdādṛhāṇo वज्रमिन्द्रोvajramindro गभस्त्योःghabhastyoḥ कषद्मेवkṣadmeva तिग्ममसनायसंtighmamasanāyasaṃ शयदहिहत्यायśyadahihatyāya संsaṃ शयतśyat | संविव्यानsaṃvivyāna ओजसाojasā शवोभिरिन्द्रśavobhirindra मज्मनाmajmanā |

तष्टेवtaṣṭeva वर्क्षंvṛkṣaṃ वनिनोvanino निni वर्श्चसिvṛścasi परश्वेवparaśveva निni वर्श्चसिvṛścasi ||
Grasping his thunderbolt with both hands, Indra made its edge most keen, for hurling, like a carving-knife for Ahi's slaughter made it keen.
Endued with majesty and strength, O Indra, and with lordly might,
Thou crashest down the trees, as when a craftsman fells, crashest them down as with an axe.
1.130.4
तवंtvaṃ वर्थाvṛthā नद्यnadya इन्द्रindra सर्तवे.अछाsartave.achā समुद्रमस्र्जोsamudramasṛjo रथानिवrathāniva वाजयतोvājayato रथानिवrathāniva | इतita ऊतीरयुञ्जतūtīrayuñjata समानमर्थमक्षितमsamānamarthamakṣitam |

धेनूरिवdhenūriva मनवेmanave विश्वदोहसोviśvadohaso जनायjanāya विश्वदोहसःviśvadohasaḥ ||
Thou, Indra, without effort hast let loose the floods to run their free course down,
like chariots, to the sea, like chariots showing forth their strength.
They, reaching hence away, have joined their strength for one eternal end,
Even as the cows who poured forth every thing for man, Yea, poured forth all things for mankind.
1.130.5
इमांimāṃ तेte वाचंvācaṃ वसूयन्तvasūyanta आयवोāyavo रथंrathaṃ नna धीरःdhīraḥ सवपातक्षिषुःsvapāatakṣiṣuḥ सुम्नायsumnāya तवामतक्षिषुःtvāmatakṣiṣuḥ | शुम्भन्तोśumbhanto जेन्यंjenyaṃ यथाyathā वाजेषुvājeṣu विप्रvipra वाजिनमvājinam |

अत्यमिवatyamiva शवसेśavase सातयेsātaye धनाdhanā विश्वाviśvā धनानिdhanāni सातयेsātaye ||
Eager for riches, men have formed for thee this song, like as a skilful craftsman fashioneth a car, so have they wrought thee to their bliss;
Adorning thee, O Singer, like a generous steed for deeds of might,
Yea, like a steed to show his strength and win the prize, that he may bear each prize away.
1.130.6
भिनतbhinat पुरोpuro नवतिमिन्द्रnavatimindra पूरवेpūrave दिवोदासायdivodāsāya महिmahi दाशुषेdāśuṣe नर्तोnṛto वज्रेणvajreṇa दाशुषेdāśuṣe नर्तोnṛto | अतिथिग्वायatithighvāya शम्बरंśambaraṃ गिरेरुग्रोवाभरतghirerughroavābharat |

महोmaho धनानिdhanāni दयमानdayamāna ओजसाojasā विश्वाviśvā धनान्योजसाdhanānyojasā ||
For Pūru thou hast shattered, Indra ninety forts, for Divodāsa thy boon servant with thy bolt, O Dancer, for thy worshipper.
For Atithigva he, the Strong, brought Śambara. from the mountain down,
Distributing the mighty treasures with his strength, parting all treasures with his strength.
1.130.7
इन्द्रःindraḥ समत्सुsamatsu यजमानमार्यंyajamānamāryaṃ परावदprāvad विश्वेषुviśveṣu शतमूतिराजिषुśatamūtirājiṣu सवर्मीळ्हेष्वाजिषुsvarmīḷheṣvājiṣu | मनवेmanave शासदव्रतानśāsadavratān तवचंtvacaṃ कर्ष्णामरन्धयतkṛṣṇāmarandhayat |

दक्षनdakṣan नna विश्वंviśvaṃ तत्र्षाणमोषतिन्यर्शसानमोषतिtatṛṣāṇamoṣatinyarśasānamoṣati ||
Indra in battles help his Āryan worshipper, he who hath hundred helps at hand in every fray, in frays that win the light of heaven.
Plaguing the lawless he gave up to Manu's seed the dusky skin;
Blazing, ’twere, he burns each covetous man away, he burns, the tyrannous away.
1.130.8
सूरश्चक्रंsūraścakraṃ परpra वर्हज्जातvṛhajjāta ओजसाojasā परपित्वेprapitve वाचमरुणोvācamaruṇo मुषायतीशानmuṣāyatīśāna आā मुषायतिmuṣāyati | उशनाuśanā यतyat परावतो.अजगन्नूतयेparāvato.ajaghannūtaye कवेkave |

सुम्नानिsumnāni विश्वाviśvā मनुषेवmanuṣeva तुर्वणिरहाturvaṇirahā विश्वेवतुर्वणिःviśvevaturvaṇiḥ ||
Waxed strong in might at dawn he tore the Sun's wheel off. Bright red, he steals away their speech, the Lord of Power, their speech he steals away from them,
As thou with eager speed, O Sage, hast come from far away to help,
As winning for thine own all happiness of men, winning all happiness each day.
1.130.9
सsa नोno नव्येभिर्व्र्षकर्मन्नुक्थैःnavyebhirvṛṣakarmannukthaiḥ पुरांpurāṃ दर्तःdartaḥ पायुभिःपाहिpāyubhiḥpāhi शग्मैःśaghmaiḥ |

दिवोदासेभिरिन्द्रdivodāsebhirindra सतवानोstavāno वाव्र्धीथाvāvṛdhīthā अहोभिरिवahobhiriva दयौःdyauḥ ||
Lauded with our new hymns, O vigorous in deed, save us with strengthening help, thou Shatterer of the Forts!
Thou, Indra, praised by Divodāsa's clansmen, as heaven grows great with days, shalt wax in glory.
