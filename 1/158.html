HYMN CLVIII. AŚVINS.

वसूvasū रुद्राrudrā पुरुमन्तूpurumantū वर्धन्ताvṛdhantā दशस्यतंdaśasyataṃ नोno वर्षणावभिष्टौvṛṣaṇāvabhiṣṭau |

दस्राdasrā हha यदyad रेक्णrekṇa औचथ्योaucathyo वांvāṃ परpra यतyat सस्राथेकवाभिरूतीsasrātheakavābhirūtī ||
YE Vasus Twain, ye Rudras full of counsel, grant us, Strong Strengtheners, when ye stand beside us,
What wealth Aucathya craves of you, great Helpers when ye come forward with no niggard succour.
1.158.1
कोko वांvāṃ दाशतdāśat सुमतयेsumataye चिदस्यैcidasyai वसूvasū यदyad धेथेdhethe नमसाnamasā पदेpade गोःghoḥ |

जिग्र्तमस्मेjighṛtamasme रेवतीःrevatīḥ पुरन्धीःpurandhīḥ कामप्रेणेवkāmapreṇeva मनसाmanasā चरन्ताcarantā ||
Who may give you aught, Vasus, for your favour, for what, at the Cow's place, ye grant through worship?
Wake for us understanding full of riches, come with a heart that will fulfil our longing.
1.158.2
युक्तोyukto हha यदyad वांvāṃ तौग्र्यायtaughryāya पेरुर्विperurvi मध्येmadhye अर्णसोarṇaso धायिdhāyi पज्रःpajraḥ |

उपupa वामवःvāmavaḥ शरणंśaraṇaṃ गमेयंghameyaṃ शूरोśūro नाज्मnājma पतयद्भिरेवैःpatayadbhirevaiḥ ||
As erst for Tugra's son your car, sea-crossing, strong, was equipped and set amid the waters,
So may I gain your shelter and protection as with winged course a hero seeks his army.
1.158.3
उपस्तुतिरौचथ्यमुरुष्येनupastutiraucathyamuruṣyen माmā मामिमेmāmime पतत्रिणीpatatriṇī विvi दुग्धामdughdhām |

माmā मामेधोmāmedho दशतयश्चितोdaśatayaścito धाकdhāk परpra यदyad वांvāṃ बद्धस्त्मनिbaddhastmani खादतिkhādati कषामkṣām ||
May this my praise preserve Ucathya's offspring: let not these Twain who fly with wings exhaust me.
Let not the wood ten times up-piled consume me, when fixed for you it bites the ground it stands on.
1.158.4
नna माmā गरनgharan नद्योnadyo मात्र्तमाmātṛtamā दासाdāsā यदींyadīṃ सुसमुब्धमवाधुःsusamubdhamavādhuḥ |

शिरोśiro यदस्यyadasya तरैतनोtraitano वितक्षतvitakṣat सवयंsvayaṃ दासdāsa उरोuro अंसावपिaṃsāvapi गधghdha ||
The most maternal streams, wherein the Dāsas cast me securely bound, have not devoured me.
When Traitana would cleave my head asunder, the Dāsa wounded his own breast and shoulders.
1.158.5
दीर्घतमाdīrghatamā मामतेयोmāmateyo जुजुर्वानjujurvān दशमेdaśame युगेyughe |

अपामर्थंapāmarthaṃ यतीनांyatīnāṃ बरह्माbrahmā भवतिbhavati सारथिःsārathiḥ ||
Dīrghatamas the son of Mamatā hath come to length of days in the tenth age of human kind.
He is the Brahman of the waters as they strive to reach their end and aim: their charioteer is he.
1.158.6
