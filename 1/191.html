HYMN CXCI. WATER. GRASS. SUN.

कङकतोkaṅkato नna कङकतो.अथोkaṅkato.atho सतीनकङकतःsatīnakaṅkataḥ |

दवावितिdvāviti पलुषीpluṣī इतिiti नयद्र्ष्टnyadṛṣṭa अलिप्सतalipsata ||
VENOMOUS, slightly venomous, or venomous aquatic worm,—
Both creatures, stinging, unobserved, with poison have infected me.
1.191.1
अद्र्ष्टानadṛṣṭān हन्त्यायत्यथोhantyāyatyatho हन्तिhanti परायतीparāyatī |

अथोatho अवघ्नतीavaghnatī हन्त्यथोhantyatho पिनष्टिpinaṣṭi पिंषतीpiṃṣatī ||
Coming, it kills the unobserved; it kills them as it goes away,
It kills them as it drives them off, and bruising bruises them to death.
1.191.2
शरासःśarāsaḥ कुशरासोkuśarāso दर्भासःdarbhāsaḥ सैर्याsairyā उतuta |

मौञ्जाmauñjā अद्र्ष्टाadṛṣṭā वैरिणाःvairiṇāḥ सर्वेsarve साकंsākaṃ नयलिप्सतnyalipsata ||
Sara grass, Darbha, Kuśara, and Sairya, Muñja, Vīraṇa,
Where all these creatures dwell unseen, with poison have infected me.
1.191.3
निni गावोghāvo गोष्ठेghoṣṭhe असदनasadan निni मर्गासोmṛghāso अविक्षतavikṣata |

निni केतवोketavo जनानांjanānāṃ नयद्र्ष्टाnyadṛṣṭā अलिप्सतalipsata ||
The cows had settled in their stalls, the beasts of prey had sought their lairs,
Extinguished were the lights of men, when things unseen infected me.
1.191.4
एतeta उu तयेtye परत्यद्र्श्रनpratyadṛśran परदोषंpradoṣaṃ तस्कराtaskarā इवiva |

अद्र्ष्टाadṛṣṭā विश्वद्र्ष्टाःviśvadṛṣṭāḥ परतिबुद्धाpratibuddhā अभूतनabhūtana ||
Or these, these reptiles, are observed, like lurking thieves at evening time.
Seers of all, themselves unseen: be therefore very vigilant.
1.191.5
दयौर्वःdyaurvaḥ पिताpitā पर्थिवीpṛthivī माताmātā सोमोsomo भरातादितिःbhrātāditiḥ सवसाsvasā |

अद्र्ष्टाadṛṣṭā विश्वद्र्ष्टास्तिष्ठतेलयताviśvadṛṣṭāstiṣṭhatelayatā सुsu कमkam ||
Heaven is your Sire, your Mother Earth, Soma your Brother, Aditi
Your Sister: seeing all, unseen, keep still and dwell ye happily.
1.191.6
येye अंस्याaṃsyā येye अङगयाःaṅghyāḥ सूचीकाsūcīkā येye परकङकताःprakaṅkatāḥ |

अद्र्ष्टाःadṛṣṭāḥ किंkiṃ चनेहcaneha वःvaḥ सर्वेsarve साकंsākaṃ निni जस्यतjasyata ||
Biters of shoulder or of limb, with needle-stings, most venomous,
Unseen, whatever ye may be, vanish together and be gone.
1.191.7
उतut पुरस्तातpurastāt सूर्यsūrya एतिeti विश्वद्र्ष्टोviśvadṛṣṭo अद्र्ष्टहाadṛṣṭahā |

अद्र्ष्टानadṛṣṭān सर्वाञsarvāñ जम्भयनjambhayan सर्वाश्चsarvāśca यातुधान्यःyātudhānyaḥ ||
Slayer of things unseen, the Sun, beheld of all, mounts, eastward, up,
Consuming all that are not seen, and evil spirits of the night.
1.191.8
उदपप्तदसौudapaptadasau सूर्यःsūryaḥ पुरुpuru विश्वानिviśvāni जूर्वनjūrvan |

आदित्यःādityaḥ पर्वतेभ्योparvatebhyo विश्वद्र्ष्टोviśvadṛṣṭo अद्र्ष्टहाadṛṣṭahā ||
There hath the Sun-God mounted up, who scorches much and everything.
Even the Āditya from the hills, all-seen, destroying things unseen.
1.191.9
सूर्येsūrye विषमाviṣamā सजामिsajāmi दर्तिंdṛtiṃ सुरावतोsurāvato गर्हेghṛhe |

सोso चिनcin नुnu नमरातिnamarāti नोno वयंvayaṃ मरामारेmarāmāre अस्यasya योजनंyojanaṃ हरिष्ठाhariṣṭhā मधुmadhu तवामधुलाtvāmadhulā चकारcakāra ||
I hang the poison in the Sun, a wine-skin in a vintner's house,
He will not die, nor shall we die: his path is far: he whom Bay Horses bear hath turned thee to sweet meath.
1.191.10
इयत्तिकाiyattikā शकुन्तिकाśakuntikā सकाsakā जघासjaghāsa तेte विषमviṣam |

सोso चिनcin नुnu ...... ||
This little bird, so very small, hath swallowed all thy poison up.
She will not die, nor shall we die: his path is far: he whom Bay Horses bear hath turned thee to sweet meath.
1.191.11
तरिःtriḥ सप्तsapta विष्पुलिङगकाviṣpuliṅghakā विषस्यviṣasya पुष्यमक्षनpuṣyamakṣan |

ताश्चिन्नुtāścinnu नna मरन्तिmaranti नोno वयंvayaṃ म...ma... ||
The three-times-seven bright sparks of fire have swallowed up the poison's strength.
They will not die, nor shall we die: his path is far: he whom Bay Horses bear hath turned thee to sweet meath.
1.191.12
नवानांnavānāṃ नवतीनांnavatīnāṃ विषस्यviṣasya रोपुषीणामropuṣīṇām |

सर्वासामग्रभंsarvāsāmaghrabhaṃ नामारेnāmāre अस्यasya यो...yo... ||
Of ninety rivers and of nine with power to stay the venom's course,—
The names of all I have secured: his path is far: he whom Bay Horses bear hath turned thee to sweet meath.
1.191.13
तरिःtriḥ सप्तsapta मयूर्यःmayūryaḥ सप्तsapta सवसारोsvasāro अग्रुवःaghruvaḥ |

तास्तेtāste विषंviṣaṃ विvi जभ्रिरjabhrira उदकंudakaṃ कुम्भिनीरिवkumbhinīriva ||
So have the peahens three-times-seven, so have the maiden Sisters Seven
Carried thy venom far away, as girls bear water in their jars.
1.191.14
इयत्तकःiyattakaḥ कुषुम्भकस्तकंkuṣumbhakastakaṃ भिनद्म्यश्मनाbhinadmyaśmanā |

ततोtato विषंviṣaṃ परpra वाव्र्तेvāvṛte पराचीरनुparācīranu संवतःsaṃvataḥ ||
The poison-insect is so small; I crush the creature with a stone.
I turn the poison hence away, departed unto distant lands.
1.191.15
कुषुम्भकस्तदब्रवीदkuṣumbhakastadabravīd गिरेःghireḥ परवर्तमानकःpravartamānakaḥ |

वर्श्चिकस्यारसंvṛścikasyārasaṃ विषमरसंviṣamarasaṃ वर्श्चिकvṛścika तेte विषमviṣam ||
Forth issuing from the mountain's side the poison-insect spake and said:
Scorpion, they venom is but weak.
1.191.16
