HYMN LXII. INDRA.

परpra मन्महेmanmahe शवसानायśavasānāya शूषमाङगूषंśūṣamāṅghūṣaṃ गिर्वणसेghirvaṇase अङगिरस्वतaṅghirasvat |

सुव्र्क्तिभिःsuvṛktibhiḥ सतुवतstuvata रग्मियायार्चामार्कंṛghmiyāyārcāmārkaṃ नरेnare विश्रुतायviśrutāya ||
LIKE Aṅgiras a gladdening laud we ponder to him who loveth song, exceeding mighty.
Let us sing glory to the far-famed Hero who must be praised with fair hymns by the singer.
1.62.1
परpra वोvo महेmahe महिmahi नमोnamo भरध्वमाङगूष्यंbharadhvamāṅghūṣyaṃ शवसानायśavasānāya सामsāma |

येनाyenā नःnaḥ पूर्वेpūrve पितरःpitaraḥ पदज्ञाpadajñā अर्चन्तोarcanto अङगिरसोaṅghiraso गाghā अविन्दनavindan ||
Unto the great bring ye great adoration, a chant with praise to him exceeding mighty,
Through whom our sires, Aṅgirases, singing praises and knowing well the places, found the cattle.
1.62.2
इन्द्रस्याङगिरसांindrasyāṅghirasāṃ चेष्टौceṣṭau विदतvidat सरमाsaramā तनयायtanayāya धासिमdhāsim |

बर्हस्पतिर्भिनदद्रिंbṛhaspatirbhinadadriṃ विददvidad गाःghāḥ समुस्रियाभिर्वावशन्तsamusriyābhirvāvaśanta नरःnaraḥ ||
When Indra and the Aṅgirases desired it, Saramā found provision for her offspring.
Bṛhaspati cleft the mountain, found the cattle: the heroes shouted with the kine in triumph.
1.62.3
सsa सुष्टुभाsuṣṭubhā सsa सतुभाstubhā सप्तsapta विप्रैःvipraiḥ सवरेणाद्रिंsvareṇādriṃ सवर्योsvaryo नवग्वैःnavaghvaiḥ |

सरण्युभिःsaraṇyubhiḥ फलिगमिन्द्रphalighamindra शक्रśakra वलंvalaṃ रवेणदरयोraveṇadarayo दशग्वैःdaśaghvaiḥ ||
Mid shout, loud shout, and roar, with the Navagvas, seven singers, hast thou, heavenly, rent the mountain;
Thou hast, with speeders, with Daśagvas, Indra, Śakra, with thunder rent obstructive Vala.
1.62.4
गर्णानोghṛṇāno अङगिरोभिर्दस्मaṅghirobhirdasma विvi वरुषसाvaruṣasā सूर्येणsūryeṇa गोभिरन्धःghobhirandhaḥ |

विvi भूम्याbhūmyā अप्रथयaprathaya इन्द्रindra सानुsānu दिवोdivo रजraja उपरमस्तभायःuparamastabhāyaḥ ||
Praised by Aṅgirases, thou, foe-destroyer, hast, with the Dawn, Sun, rays, dispelled the darkness.
Thou Indra, hast spread out the earth's high ridges, and firmly fixed the region under heaven.
1.62.5
तदुtadu परयक्षतममस्यprayakṣatamamasya कर्मkarma दस्मस्यdasmasya चारुतममस्तिcārutamamasti दंसःdaṃsaḥ |

उपह्वरेupahvare यदुपराyaduparā अपिन्वनapinvan मध्वर्णसोmadhvarṇaso नद्यश्चतस्रःnadyaścatasraḥ ||
This is the deed most worthy of all honour, the fairest marvel of the Wonder-Worker,
That, nigh where heaven bends down, he made four rivers flow full with waves that carry down sweet water.
1.62.6
दविताdvitā विvi वव्रेvavre सनजाsanajā सनीळेsanīḷe अयास्यayāsya सतवमानेभिरर्कैःstavamānebhirarkaiḥ |

भगोbhagho नna मेनेmene परमेparame वयोमन्नधारयदvyomannadhārayad रोदसीrodasī सुदंसाःsudaṃsāḥ ||
Unwearied, won with lauding hymns, he parted of old the ancient Pair, united ever.
In highest sky like Bhaga, he the doer of marvels set both Dames and earth and heaven.
1.62.7
सनादsanād दिवंdivaṃ परिpari भूमाbhūmā विरूपेvirūpe पुनर्भुवाpunarbhuvā युवतीyuvatī सवेभिरेवैःsvebhirevaiḥ |

कर्ष्णेभिरक्तोषाkṛṣṇebhiraktoṣā रुशद्भिर्वपुर्भिराruśadbhirvapurbhirā चरतोcarato अन्यान्याanyānyā ||
Still born afresh, young Dames, each in her manner, unlike in hue, the Pair in alternation
Round heaven and earth from ancient time have travelled, Night with her dark limbs, Dawn with limbs of splendour.
1.62.8
सनेमिsanemi सख्यंsakhyaṃ सवपस्यमानःsvapasyamānaḥ सूनुर्दाधारsūnurdādhāra शवसाśavasā सुदंसाःsudaṃsāḥ |

आमासुāmāsu चिदcid दधिषेdadhiṣe पक्वमन्तःpakvamantaḥ पयःpayaḥ कर्ष्णासुkṛṣṇāsu रुशदruśad रोहिणीषुrohiṇīṣu ||
Rich in good actions, skilled in operation, the Son with might maintains his perfect friendship.
Thou in the raw cows, black of hue or ruddy, storest the ripe milk glossy white in colour.
1.62.9
सनातsanāt सनीलाsanīlā अवनीरवाताavanīravātā वरताvratā रक्षन्तेrakṣante अम्र्ताःamṛtāḥ सहोभिःsahobhiḥ |

पुरूpurū सहस्राsahasrā जनयोjanayo नna पत्नीर्दुवस्यन्तिpatnīrduvasyanti सवसारोsvasāro अह्रयाणमahrayāṇam ||
Their paths, of old connected, rest uninjured; they with great might preserve the immortal statutes.
For many thousand holy works the Sisters wait on the haughty Lord like wives and matrons.
1.62.10
सनायुवोsanāyuvo नमसाnamasā नव्योnavyo अर्कैर्वसूयवोarkairvasūyavo मतयोmatayo दस्मdasma दद्रुःdadruḥ |

पतिंpatiṃ नna पत्नीरुशतीरुशन्तंpatnīruśatīruśantaṃ सप्र्शन्तिspṛśanti तवाtvā शवसावन्मनीषाःśavasāvanmanīṣāḥ ||
Thoughts ancient, seeking wealth, with adoration, with newest lauds have sped to thee, O Mighty.
As yearning wives cleave to their yearning husband, so cleave our hymns to thee, O Lord most potent.
1.62.11
सनादेवsanādeva तवtava रायोrāyo गभस्तौghabhastau नna कषीयन्तेkṣīyante नोपnopa दस्यन्तिdasyanti दस्मdasma |

दयुमानसिdyumānasi करतुमानिन्द्रkratumānindra धीरःdhīraḥ शिक्षाśikṣā शचीवस्तवśacīvastava नःnaḥ शचीभिःśacībhiḥ ||
Strong God, the riches which thy hands have holden from days of old have perished not nor wasted.
Splendid art thou, O Indra, wise, unbending: strengthen us with might, O Lord of Power.
1.62.12
सनायतेsanāyate गोतमghotama इन्द्रindra नव्यमतक्षदnavyamatakṣad बरह्मbrahma हरियोजनायhariyojanāya |

सुनीथायsunīthāya नःnaḥ शवसानśavasāna नोधाःnodhāḥ परातरprātar मक्षूmakṣū धियावसुरdhiyāvasur जगम्यात||jaghamyāt||
O mighty Indra, Gotama's son Nodhas hath fashioned this new prayer to thee Eternal,
Sure leader, yoker of the Tawny Coursers. May he, enriched with prayer, come soon and early.
