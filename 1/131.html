HYMN CXXXI. INDRA.

इन्द्रायindrāya हिhi दयौरसुरोdyaurasuro अनम्नतेन्द्रायanamnatendrāya महीmahī पर्थिवीpṛthivī वरीमभिर्द्युम्नसाताvarīmabhirdyumnasātā वरीमभिःvarīmabhiḥ | इन्द्रंindraṃ विश्वेviśve सजोषसोsajoṣaso देवासोdevāso दधिरेdadhire पुरःpuraḥ |

इन्द्रायindrāya विश्वाviśvā सवनानिsavanāni मानुषाmānuṣā रातानिrātāni सन्तुsantu मानुषाmānuṣā ||
To Indra Dyaus the Asura hath bowed him down, to Indra mighty Earth with wide-extending tracts, to win the light, with wide-spread tracts.
All Gods of one accord have set Indra in front preeminent.
For Indra all libations must be set apart, all man's libations set apart.
1.131.1
विश्वेषुviśveṣu हिhi तवाtvā सवनेषुsavaneṣu तुञ्जतेtuñjate समानमेकंsamānamekaṃ वर्षमण्यवःvṛṣamaṇyavaḥ पर्थकpṛthak सवःsvaḥ सनिष्यवःsaniṣyavaḥ पर्थकpṛthak | तंtaṃ तवाtvā नावंnāvaṃ नna पर्षणिंparṣaṇiṃ शूषस्यśūṣasya धुरिdhuri धीमहिdhīmahi |

इन्द्रंindraṃ नna यज्ञैश्चितयन्तyajñaiścitayanta आयवāyava सतोमेभिरिन्द्रमायवःstomebhirindramāyavaḥ ||
In all libations men with hero spirit urge the Universal One, each seeking several light, each fain to win the light apart.
Thee, furthering like a ship, will we set to the chariot-pole of strength,
As men who win with sacrifices Indra's thought, men who win Indra with their lauds.
1.131.2
विvi तवाtvā ततस्रेtatasre मिथुनाmithunā अवस्यवोavasyavo वरजस्यvrajasya साताsātā गव्यस्यghavyasya निःस्र्जःniḥsṛjaḥ सक्षन्तsakṣanta इन्द्रindra निःस्र्जःniḥsṛjaḥ | यदyad गव्यन्ताghavyantā दवाdvā जनाjanā सवर्यन्ताsvaryantā समूहसिsamūhasi |

आविषāviṣ करिक्रदkarikrad वर्षणंvṛṣaṇaṃ सचाभुवंsacābhuvaṃ वज्रमिन्द्रvajramindra सचाभुवमsacābhuvam ||
Couples desirous of thine aid are storming thee, pouring their presents forth to win a stall of kine, pouring gifts, Indra, seeking thee.
When two men seeking spoil or heaven thou bringest face to face in war,
Thou showest, Indra, then the bolt thy constant friend, the Bull that ever waits on thee.
1.131.3
विदुषviduṣ टेṭe अस्यasya वीर्यस्यvīryasya पूरवःpūravaḥ पुरोpuro यदिन्द्रyadindra शारदीरवातिरःśāradīravātiraḥ सासहानोsāsahāno अवातिरःavātiraḥ | शासस्तमिन्द्रśāsastamindra मर्त्यमयज्युंmartyamayajyuṃ शवससśavasas पतेpate |

महीममुष्णाःmahīmamuṣṇāḥ पर्थिवीमिमाpṛthivīmimā अपोapo मन्दसानmandasāna इमाimā अपःapaḥ ||
This thine heroic power men of old time have known, wherewith thou breakest down, Indra, autumnal forts, breakest them down with conquering might.
Thou hast chastised, O Indra, Lord of Strength, the man who worships not,
And made thine own this great earth and these water-floods; with joyous heart these
water-floods
.
1.131.4
आदितādit तेte अस्यasya वीर्यस्यvīryasya चर्किरनcarkiran मदेषुmadeṣu वर्षन्नुशिजोvṛṣannuśijo यदाविथyadāvitha सखीयतोsakhīyato यदाविथyadāvitha | चकर्थcakartha कारमेभ्यःkāramebhyaḥ पर्तनासुpṛtanāsu परवन्तवpravantava |

तेte अन्याम-अन्यांanyām-anyāṃ नद्यंnadyaṃ सनिष्णतsaniṣṇata शरवस्यन्तःśravasyantaḥ सनिष्णतsaniṣṇata ||
And they have bruited far this hero-might when thou, O Strong One, in thy joy helpest thy suppliants, who sought to win thee for their Friend.
Their battle-cry thou madest sound victorious in the shocks of war.
One stream after another have they gained from thee, eager for glory have they gained.
1.131.5
उतोuto नोno अस्याasyā उषसोuṣaso जुषेतjuṣeta हयर्कस्यhyarkasya बोधिbodhi हविषोhaviṣo हवीमभिःhavīmabhiḥ सवर्षाताsvarṣātā हवीमभिःhavīmabhiḥ | यदिन्द्रyadindra हन्तवेhantave मर्धोmṛdho वर्षाvṛṣā वज्रिञ्चिकेतसिvajriñciketasi |

आā मेme अस्यasya वेधसोvedhaso नवीयसोnavīyaso मन्मmanma शरुधिśrudhi नवीयसःnavīyasaḥ ||
Also this morn may he be well inclined to us, mark at our call our offerings and our song of praise, our call that we may win the light.
As thou, O Indra Thunder-armed, wilt, as the Strong One, slay the foe,
Listen thou to the prayer of me a later sage, hear thou a later sage's prayer.
1.131.6
तवंtvaṃ तमिन्द्रtamindra वाव्र्धानोvāvṛdhāno अस्मयुरमित्रयन्तंasmayuramitrayantaṃ तुविजातtuvijāta मर्त्यंmartyaṃ वज्रेणvajreṇa शूरśūra मर्त्यमmartyam | जहिjahi योyo नोno अघायतिaghāyati शर्णुष्वśṛṇuṣva सुश्रवस्तमःsuśravastamaḥ |

रिष्टंriṣṭaṃ नna यामन्नपyāmannapa भूतुbhūtu दुर्मतिर्विश्वापdurmatirviśvāpa भूतुbhūtu दुर्मतिःdurmatiḥ ||
O Indra, waxen strong and well-inclined to us, thou very mighty, slay the man that is our foe, slay the man, Hero! with thy bolt.
Slay thou the man who injures us: hear thou, as readiest, to hear.
Far be malignity, like mischief on the march, afar be all malignity.
