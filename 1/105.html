HYMN CV. VIŚVEDEVAS.

चन्द्रमाcandramā अप्स्वन्तराapsvantarā सुपर्णोsuparṇo धावतेdhāvate दिविdivi |

नna वोvo हिरण्यनेमयःhiraṇyanemayaḥ पदंpadaṃ विन्दन्तिvindanti विद्युतोvidyuto वित्तंvittaṃ मेme अस्यasya रोदसीrodasī ||
WITHIN the waters runs the Moon, he with the beauteous wings in heaven.
Ye lightnings with your golden wheels, men find not your abiding-place. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.1
अर्थमिदarthamid वाvā उu अर्थिनarthina आā जायाjāyā युवतेyuvate पतिमpatim |

तुञ्जातेtuñjāte वर्ष्ण्यंvṛṣṇyaṃ पयःpayaḥ परिदायparidāya रसंrasaṃ दुहेduhe वित्तम...vittam... ||
Surely men crave and gain their wish. Close to her husband clings the wife.
And, in embraces intertwined, both give and take the bliss of love. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.2
मोmo षुṣu देवdeva अदःadaḥ सवरवsvarava पादिpādi दिवसdivas परिpari |

माmā सोम्यस्यsomyasya शम्भुवःśambhuvaḥ शूनेśūne भूमbhūma कदाkadā चनcana वित्तम...vittam... ||
O never may that light, ye Gods, fall from its station in the sky.
Ne’er fail us one like Soma sweet, the spring of our felicity. Mark this my woe
,
ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.3
यज्ञंyajñaṃ पर्छाम्यवमंpṛchāmyavamaṃ सsa तदtad दूतोdūto विvi वोचतिvocati |

कवkva रतंṛtaṃ पूर्व्यंpūrvyaṃ गतंghataṃ कस्तदkastad बिभर्तिbibharti नूतनोnūtano वि...vi... ||
I ask the last of sacrifice. As envoy he shall tell it forth.
Where is the ancient law divine? Who is its new diffuser now? Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.4
अमीamī येye देवाdevā सथनsthana तरिष्वाtriṣvā रोचनेrocane दिवःdivaḥ |

कदkad वva रतंṛtaṃ कदन्र्तंkadanṛtaṃ कवkva परत्नाpratnā वva आहुतिर्वि...āhutirvi... ||
Ye Gods who yonder have your home in the three lucid realms of heaven,
What count ye truth and what untruth? Where is mine ancient call on you? Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.5
कदkad वva रतस्यṛtasya धर्णसिdharṇasi कदkad वरुणस्यvaruṇasya चक्षणमcakṣaṇam |

कदर्यम्णोkadaryamṇo महसmahas पथातिpathāti करामेमkrāmema दूढ्योdūḍhyo वि...vi... ||
What is your firm support of Law? What Varuṇa's observant eye?
How may we pass the wicked on the path of mighty Aryaman? Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.6
अहमaham सोso अस्मिasmi यःyaḥ पुराpurā सुतेsute वदामिvadāmi कानिkāni चितcit |

तंtaṃ माmā वयन्त्याध्योvyantyādhyo वर्कोvṛko नna तर्ष्णजंtṛṣṇajaṃ मर्गंmṛghaṃ वि...vi... ||
I am the man who sang of old full many a laud when Soma flowed.
Yet torturing cares consume me as the wolf assails the thirsty deer. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.7
संsaṃ माmā तपन्त्यभितःtapantyabhitaḥ सपत्नीरिवsapatnīriva पर्शवःparśavaḥ |

मूषोmūṣo नna शिश्नाśiśnā वयदन्तिvyadanti माध्यmādhya सतोतारंstotāraṃ तेte शतक्रतोśatakrato वि...vi... ||
Like rival wives on every side enclosing ribs oppress me sore.
O Śatakratu, biting cares devour me, singer of thy praise, as rats devour the weaver's threads. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.8
अमीamī येye सप्तsapta रश्मयस्तत्राraśmayastatrā मेme नाभिरातताnābhirātatā |

तरितस्तदtritastad वेदाप्त्यःvedāptyaḥ सsa जामित्वायjāmitvāya रेभतिrebhati वि...vi... ||
Where those seven rays are shining, thence my home and family extend.
This Tṛta Āptya knoweth well, and speaketh out for brotherhood. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.9
अमीamī येye पञ्चोक्षणोpañcokṣaṇo मध्येmadhye तस्थुर्महोtasthurmaho दिवःdivaḥ |

देवत्राdevatrā नुnu परवाच्यंpravācyaṃ सध्रीचीनाsadhrīcīnā निni वाव्र्तुर्वि...vāvṛturvi... ||
May those five Bulls which stand on high full in the midst of mighty heaven,
Having together swiftly borne my praises to the Gods, return. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.10
सुपर्णाsuparṇā एतeta आसतेāsate मध्यmadhya आरोधनेārodhane दिवःdivaḥ |

तेte सेधन्तिsedhanti पथोpatho वर्कंvṛkaṃ तरन्तंtarantaṃ यह्वतीरपोyahvatīrapo वि...vi... ||
High in the mid ascent of heaven those Birds of beauteous pinion sit.
Back from his path they drive the wolf as he would cross the restless floods. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.11
नव्यंnavyaṃ तदुक्थ्यंtadukthyaṃ हितंhitaṃ देवासःdevāsaḥ सुप्रवाचनमsupravācanam |

रतमर्षन्तिṛtamarṣanti सिन्धवःsindhavaḥ सत्यंsatyaṃ तातानtātāna सूर्योsūryo वि...vi... ||
Firm is this new-wrought hymn of praise, and meet to be told forth, O Gods.
The flowing of the floods is Law, Truth is the Sun's extended light. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.12
अग्नेaghne तवtava तयदुक्थ्यंtyadukthyaṃ देवेष्वस्त्याप्यमdeveṣvastyāpyam |

सsa नःnaḥ सत्तोsatto मनुष्वदाmanuṣvadā देवानdevān यक्षिyakṣi विदुष्टरोviduṣṭaro वि...vi... ||
Worthy of laud, O Agni, is that kinship which thou hast with Gods.
Here seat thee like a man: most wise, bring thou the Gods for sacrifice. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.13
सत्तोsatto होताhotā मनुष्वदाmanuṣvadā देवानछाdevānachā विदुष्टरःviduṣṭaraḥ |

अग्निर्हव्याaghnirhavyā सुषूदतिsuṣūdati देवोdevo देवेषुdeveṣu मेधिरोmedhiro वि...vi... ||
Here seated, man-like as a priest shall wisest Agni to the Gods
Speed onward our oblations, God among the Gods, intelligent. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.14
बरह्माbrahmā कर्णोतिkṛṇoti वरुणोvaruṇo गातुविदंghātuvidaṃ तमीमहेtamīmahe |

वयूर्णोतिvyūrṇoti हर्दाhṛdā मतिंmatiṃ नव्योnavyo जायतांjāyatāṃ रतंṛtaṃ वि...vi... ||
Varuṇa makes the holy prayer. To him who finds the path we pray.
He in the heart reveals his thought. Let sacred worship rise anew. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.15
असौasau यःyaḥ पन्थाpanthā आदित्योādityo दिविdivi परवाच्यंpravācyaṃ कर्तःkṛtaḥ |

नna सsa देवाdevā अतिक्रमेatikrame तंtaṃ मर्तासोmartāso नna पश्यथpaśyatha वि...vi... ||
That pathway of the Sun in heaven, made to be highly glorified,
Is not to be transgressed, O Gods. O mortals, ye behold it not. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.16
तरितःtritaḥ कूपे.अवहितोkūpe.avahito देवानdevān हवतhavata ऊतयेūtaye |

तच्छुश्रावtacchuśrāva बर्हस्पतिःbṛhaspatiḥ कर्ण्वन्नंहूरणादुरुkṛṇvannaṃhūraṇāduru वि...vi... ||
Tṛta, when buried in the well, calls on the Gods to succour him.
That call of his Bṛhaspati heard and released him from distress. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.17
अरुणोaruṇo माmā सक्र्दsakṛd वर्कःvṛkaḥ पथाpathā यन्तंyantaṃ ददर्शdadarśa हिhi |

उज्जिहीतेujjihīte निचाय्याnicāyyā तष्टेवtaṣṭeva पर्ष्ट्यामयीpṛṣṭyāmayī वि...vi... ||
A ruddy wolf beheld me once, as I was faring on my path.
He, like a carpenter whose back is aching crouched and slunk away. Mark this my woe, ye Earth and Heaven.
1.105.18
एनाङगूषेणenāṅghūṣeṇa वयमिन्द्रवन्तो.अभिvayamindravanto.abhi षयामṣyāma वर्जनेvṛjane सर्ववीराःsarvavīrāḥ |

तनtan नोno ...... ||
Through this our song may we, allied with Indra, with all our heroes conquer in the battle.
This prayer of ours may Varuṇa grant, and Mitra, and Aditi and Sindhu, Earth and Heaven.
