HYMN LXXX. DAWN.

परतिprati सतोमेभिरुषसंstomebhiruṣasaṃ वसिष्ठाvasiṣṭhā गीर्भिर्विप्रासःghīrbhirviprāsaḥ परथमाprathamā अबुध्रनabudhran |

विवर्तयन्तींvivartayantīṃ रजसीrajasī समन्तेsamante आविष्क्र्ण्वतींāviṣkṛṇvatīṃ भुवनानिbhuvanāni विश्वाviśvā ||
THE priests, Vasisthas, are the first awakened to welcome Uṣas with their songs and praises,
Who makes surrounding regions part asunder,and shows apparent all existing creatures.
7.80.1
एषाeṣā सयाsyā नव्यमायुर्दधानाnavyamāyurdadhānā गूढ्वीghūḍhvī तमोtamo जयोतिषोषाबोधिjyotiṣoṣāabodhi |

अग्रaghra एतिeti युवतिरह्रयाणाyuvatirahrayāṇā पराचिकिततprācikitat सूर्यंsūryaṃ यज्ञमग्निमyajñamaghnim ||
Giving fresh life when she hath hid the darkness, this Dawn hath wakened there with new-born lustre.
Youthful and unrestrained she cometh forward: she hath turned thoughts to Sun and fire and worship.
7.80.2
अश्वावतीर्गोमतीर्नaśvāvatīrghomatīrna उषासोuṣāso ...... ||
May blessed Mornings shine on us for ever, with wealth of kine, of horses, and of heroes,
Streaming with all abundance, pouring fatness. Preserve us evermore, ye Gods, with blessings.
