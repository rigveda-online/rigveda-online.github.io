HYMN XL. INDRA. SŪRYA. ATRI.

आā याह्यyāhy अद्रिभिःadribhiḥ सुतंsutaṃ सोमंsomaṃ सोमपतेsomapate पिबpiba |

वर्षन्नvṛṣann इन्द्रindra वर्षभिरvṛṣabhir वर्त्रहन्तमvṛtrahantama ||
COME thou to what the stones have pressed, drink Soma, O thou Soma's Lord,
Indra best Vṛtra-slayer Strong One, with the Strong.
5.40.1
वर्षाvṛṣā गरावाghrāvā वर्षाvṛṣā मदोmado वर्षाvṛṣā सोमोsomo अयंayaṃ सुतःsutaḥ |

वर्षन्नvṛṣann इन्द्रindra वर्षभिरvṛṣabhir वर्त्रहन्तमvṛtrahantama ||
Strong is the stone, the draught is strong, strong is this Soma that is pressed,
Indra, best Vṛtra-slayer, Strong One with the Strong.
5.40.2
वर्षाvṛṣā तवाtvā वर्षणंvṛṣaṇaṃ हुवेhuve वज्रिञvajriñ चित्राभिरcitrābhir ऊतिभिःūtibhiḥ |

वर्षन्नvṛṣann इन्द्रindra वर्षभिरvṛṣabhir वर्त्रहन्तमvṛtrahantama ||
As strong I call on thee the Strong, O Thunder-armed, with various aids,
Indra, best Vṛtra-slayer, Strong One with the Strong.
5.40.3
रजीषीṛjīṣī वज्रीvajrī वर्षभसvṛṣabhas तुराषाटturāṣāṭ छुष्मीchuṣmī राजाrājā वर्त्रहाvṛtrahā सोमपावाsomapāvā |

युक्त्वाyuktvā हरिभ्यामharibhyām उपupa यासदyāsad अर्वाङarvāṅ माध्यंदिनेmādhyaṃdine सवनेsavane मत्सदmatsad इन्द्रःindraḥ ||
Impetuous, Thunderer, Strong, quelling the mighty, King, potent, Vṛtra-slayer, Soma-drinker,
May he come hither with his yoked Bay Horses; may Indra gladden him at the noon libation.
5.40.4
यतyat तवाtvā सूर्यsūrya सवर्भानुसsvarbhānus तमसाविध्यदtamasāvidhyad आसुरःāsuraḥ |

अक्षेत्रविदakṣetravid यथाyathā मुग्धोmughdho भुवनान्यbhuvanāny अदीधयुःadīdhayuḥ ||
O Sūrya, when the Asura's descendant Svarbhanu, pierced thee through and through with darkness,
All creatures looked like one who is bewildered, who knoweth not the place where he is standing.
5.40.5
सवर्भानोरsvarbhānor अधadha यदyad इन्द्रindra मायाmāyā अवोavo दिवोdivo वर्तमानाvartamānā अवाहनavāhan |

गूळ्हंghūḷhaṃ सूर्यंsūryaṃ तमसापव्रतेनtamasāpavratena तुरीयेणturīyeṇa बरह्मणाविन्ददbrahmaṇāvindad अत्रिःatriḥ ||
What time thou smotest down Svarbhanu's magic that spread itself beneath the sky, O Indra,
By his fourth sacred prayer Atri disoovered Sūrya concealed in gloom that stayed his function.
5.40.6
माmā मामmām इमंimaṃ तवtava सन्तमsantam अत्रatra इरस्याirasyā दरुग्धोdrughdho भियसाbhiyasā निni गारीतghārīt |

तवमtvam मित्रोmitro असिasi सत्यराधासsatyarādhās तौtau मेहावतंmehāvataṃ वरुणशvaruṇaś चca राजाrājā ||
Let not the oppressor with this dread, through anger swallow me up, for I am thine, O Atri.
Mitra art thou, the sender of true blessings: thou and King Varuṇa be both my helpers.
5.40.7
गराव्णोghrāvṇo बरह्माbrahmā युयुजानःyuyujānaḥ सपर्यनsaparyan कीरिणाkīriṇā देवानdevān नमसोपशिक्षनnamasopaśikṣan |

अत्रिःatriḥ सूर्यस्यsūryasya दिविdivi चक्षुरcakṣur आधातādhāt सवर्भानोरsvarbhānor अपapa मायाmāyā अघुक्षतaghukṣat ||
The Brahman Atri, as he set the press-stones, serving the Gods with praise and adoration,
Established in the heaven the eye of Sūrya, and caused Svarbhanu's magic arts to vanish.
5.40.8
यंyaṃ वैvai सूर्यंsūryaṃ सवर्भानुसsvarbhānus तमसाविध्यदtamasāvidhyad आसुरःāsuraḥ |

अत्रयसatrayas तमtam अन्वanv अविन्दनavindan नह्यnahy अन्येanye अशक्नुवनaśaknuvan ||
The Atris found the Sun again, him whom Svarbhanu of the brood
Of Asuras had pierced with gloom. This none besides had power to do.
5.40.9
