HYMN XXXIV. INDRA.

अजातशत्रुमajātaśatrum अजराajarā सवर्वत्यsvarvaty अनुanu सवधामिताsvadhāmitā दस्ममdasmam ईयतेīyate |

सुनोतनsunotana पचतpacata बरह्मवाहसेbrahmavāhase पुरुष्टुतायpuruṣṭutāya परतरंprataraṃ दधातनdadhātana ||
BOUNDLESS and wasting not, the heavenly food of Gods goes to the foeless One, doer of wondrous deeds.
Press out, make ready, offer gifts with special zeal to him whom many laud, accepter of the prayer.
5.34.1
आā यःyaḥ सोमेनsomena जठरमjaṭharam अपिप्रतामन्दतapipratāmandata मघवाmaghavā मध्वोmadhvo अन्धसःandhasaḥ |

यदyad ईमīm मर्गायmṛghāya हन्तवेhantave महावधःmahāvadhaḥ सहस्रभ्र्ष्टिमsahasrabhṛṣṭim उशनाuśanā वधंvadhaṃ यमतyamat ||
He who filled full his belly with the Soma's juice, Maghavan, was delighted with the meath's sweet draught,
When Uśanā, that he might slay the monstrous beast, gave him the mighty weapon with a thousand points.
5.34.2
योyo अस्मैasmai घरंसghraṃsa उतuta वाvā यya ऊधनिūdhani सोमंsomaṃ सुनोतिsunoti भवतिbhavati दयुमांdyumāṃ अहaha |

अपापapāpa शक्रसśakras ततनुष्टिमtatanuṣṭim ऊहतिūhati तनूशुभ्रमtanūśubhram मघवाmaghavā यःyaḥ कवासखःkavāsakhaḥ ||
Illustrious is the man whoever presseth out Soma for him in sunshine or in cloud and rain.
The mighty Maghavan who is the sage's Friend advanceth more and more his beauteous progeny.
5.34.3
यस्यावधीतyasyāvadhīt पितरंpitaraṃ यस्यyasya मातरंmātaraṃ यस्यyasya शक्रोśakro भरातरंbhrātaraṃ नातnāta ईषतेīṣate |

वेतीदvetīd वv अस्यasya परयताprayatā यतंकरोyataṃkaro नna किल्बिषादkilbiṣād ईषतेīṣate वस्वvasva आकरःākaraḥ ||
The Strong God doth not flee away from him whose sire, whose mother or whose brother he hath done to death.
He, the Avenger, seeketh this man's offered gifts: this God, the source of riches, doth not flee from sin.
5.34.4
नna पञ्चभिरpañcabhir दशभिरdaśabhir वष्ट्यvaṣṭy आरभंārabhaṃ नासुन्वताnāsunvatā सचतेsacate पुष्यताpuṣyatā चनcana |

जिनातिjināti वेदved अमुयाamuyā हन्तिhanti वाvā धुनिरdhunir आā देवयुमdevayum भजतिbhajati गोमतिghomati वरजेvraje ||
He seeks no enterprise with five or ten to aid, nor stays with him who pours no juice though prospering well.
The Shaker conquers or slays in this way or that, and to the pious gives a stable full of kine.
5.34.5
वित्वक्षणःvitvakṣaṇaḥ सम्र्तौsamṛtau चक्रमासजोcakramāsajo ऽसुन्वतो'sunvato विषुणःviṣuṇaḥ सुन्वतोsunvato वर्धःvṛdhaḥ |

इन्द्रोindro विश्वस्यviśvasya दमिताdamitā विभीषणोvibhīṣaṇo यथावशंyathāvaśaṃ नयतिnayati दासमdāsam आर्यःāryaḥ ||
Exceeding strong in war he stays the chariot wheel, and, hating him who pours not, prospers him who pours.
Indra the terrible, tamer of every man, as Ārya leads away the Dāsa at his will.
5.34.6
समsam ईमīm पणेरpaṇer अजतिajati भोजनमbhojanam मुषेmuṣe विvi दाशुषेdāśuṣe भजतिbhajati सूनरंsūnaraṃ वसुvasu |

दुर्गेdurghe चनcana धरियतेdhriyate विश्वviśva आā पुरुpuru जनोjano योyo अस्यasya तविषीमtaviṣīm अचुक्रुधतacukrudhat ||
He gathers up for plunder all the niggard’s gear: excellent wealth he gives to him who offers gifts.
Not even in wide stronghold may all the folk stand firm who have provoked to anger his surpassing might.
5.34.7
संsaṃ यजyaj जनौjanau सुधनौsudhanau विश्वशर्धसावviśvaśardhasāv अवेदaved इन्द्रोindro मघवाmaghavā गोषुghoṣu शुभ्रिषुśubhriṣu |

युजंyujaṃ हयhy अन्यमanyam अक्र्तakṛta परवेपन्यpravepany उदud ईंīṃ गव्यंghavyaṃ सर्जतेsṛjate सत्वभिरsatvabhir धुनिःdhuniḥ ||
When Indra Maghavan hath marked two wealthy men fighting for beauteous cows with all their followers,
He who stirs all things takes one as his close ally, and, Shaker, with his Heroes, sends the kine to him.
5.34.8
सहस्रसामsahasrasām आग्निवेशिंāghniveśiṃ गर्णीषेghṛṇīṣe शत्रिमśatrim अग्नaghna उपमांupamāṃ केतुमketum अर्यःaryaḥ |

तस्माtasmā आपःāpaḥ संयतःsaṃyataḥ पीपयन्तpīpayanta तस्मिनtasmin कषत्रमkṣatram अमवतamavat तवेषमtveṣam अस्तुastu ||
Agni! I laud the liberal Agnivesi, Satri the type and standard of the pious.
May the collected waters yield him plenty, and his be powerful and bright dominion.
5.34.9
