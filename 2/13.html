HYMN XIII. INDRA.

रतुर्जनित्रीṛturjanitrī तस्याtasyā अपसapas परिpari मक्षूmakṣū जातjāta आविशदāviśad यासुyāsu वर्धतेvardhate |

तदाहनाtadāhanā अभवतabhavat पिप्युषीpipyuṣī पयो.अंशोःpayo.aṃśoḥ पीयूषंpīyūṣaṃ परथमंprathamaṃ तदुक्थ्यमtadukthyam ||
THE Season was the parent, and when born therefrom it entered rapidly the floods wherein it grows.
Thence was it full of sap, streaming with milky juice: the milk of the plant's stalk is chief and meet for lauds.
2.13.1
सध्रीमाsadhrīmā यन्तिyanti परिpari बिभ्रतीःbibhratīḥ पयोpayo विश्वप्स्न्यायviśvapsnyāya परpra भरन्तbharanta भोजनमbhojanam |

समानोsamāno अध्वाadhvā परवतामनुष्यदेpravatāmanuṣyade यस्ताक्र्णोःyastākṛṇoḥ परथमंprathamaṃ सास्युक्थ्यःsāsyukthyaḥ ||
They come trooping together bearing milk to him, and bring him sustenance who gives support to all.
The way is common for the downward streams to flow. Thou who didst these things first art worthy of our lauds.
2.13.2
अन्वेकोanveko वदतिvadati यदyad ददातिdadāti तदtad रूपाrūpā मिननminan तदपाtadapā एकeka ईयतेīyate |

विश्वाviśvā एकस्यekasya विनुदस्तितिक्षतेvinudastitikṣate यस्ताक्र...yastākṛ... ||
One priest announces what the institutor gives: one, altering the forms, zealously plies his task,
The third corrects the imperfections left by each. Thou who didst these things first art worthy of our lauds.
2.13.3
परजाभ्यःprajābhyaḥ पुष्टिंpuṣṭiṃ विभजन्तvibhajanta आसतेāsate रयिमिवrayimiva पर्ष्ठंpṛṣṭhaṃ परभवन्तमायतेprabhavantamāyate |

असिन्वनasinvan दंष्ट्रैःdaṃṣṭraiḥ पितुरत्तिpituratti भोजनंbhojanaṃ यस्ताक्र...yastākṛ... ||
Dealing out food unto their people there they sit, like wealth to him who comes, more than the back can bear.
Greedily with his teeth he eats the master's food. Thou who didst these things first art worthy of our lauds.
2.13.4
अधाक्र्णोःadhākṛṇoḥ पर्थिवींpṛthivīṃ सन्द्र्शेsandṛśe दिवेdive योyo धौतीनामहिहन्नारिणकdhautīnāmahihannāriṇak पथःpathaḥ |

तंtaṃ तवाtvā सतोमेभिरुदभिर्नstomebhirudabhirna वाजिनंvājinaṃ देवंdevaṃ देवाdevā अजननajanan सास्युक्थ्यःsāsyukthyaḥ ||
Thou hast created earth to look upon the sky: thou, slaying Ahi, settest free the river's paths.
Thee, such, a God, the Gods have quickened with their lauds, even as a steed with waters: meet for praise art thou.
2.13.5
योyo भोजनंbhojanaṃ चca दयसेdayase चca वर्धनमार्द्रादाvardhanamārdrādā शुष्कंśuṣkaṃ मधुमदmadhumad दुदोहिथdudohitha |

सsa शेवधिंśevadhiṃ निni दधिषेdadhiṣe विवस्वतिvivasvati विश्वस्यैकviśvasyaika ईशिषेīśiṣe सास्यु.sāsyu. ||
Thou givest increase, thou dealest to us our food: thou milkest from the moist the dry, the rich in sweets.
Thou by the worshipper layest thy precious store: thou art sole Lord of all. Meet for our praise art thou.
2.13.6
यःyaḥ पुष्पिणीश्चpuṣpiṇīśca परस्वश्चprasvaśca धर्मणाधिdharmaṇādhi दानेdāne वयवनीरधारयःvyavanīradhārayaḥ |

यश्चासमाyaścāsamā अजनोajano दिद्युतोdidyuto दिवdiva उरुरूर्वानभितःururūrvānabhitaḥ स.s. उ.u. ||
Thou who hast spread abroad the streams by stablished law, and in the field the plants that blossom and bear seed;
Thou who hast made the matchless lightnings of the sky,—vast, compassing vast realms, meet for our praise art thou.
2.13.7
योyo नार्मरंnārmaraṃ सहवसुंsahavasuṃ निहन्तवेnihantave पर्क्षायpṛkṣāya चca दासवेशायdāsaveśāya चावहःcāvahaḥ |

ऊर्जयन्त्याūrjayantyā अपरिविष्टमास्यमुतैवाद्यapariviṣṭamāsyamutaivādya पुरुक्र्त्स.purukṛts. उ.u. ||
Who broughtest Nārmara with all his wealth, for sake of food, to slay him that the fiends might be destroyed,
Broughtest the face unclouded of the strengthening one, performing much even now, worthy art thou of praise.
2.13.8
शतंśataṃ वाvā यस्यyasya दशdaśa साकमाद्यsākamādya एकस्यekasya शरुष्टौśruṣṭau यदyad धचोदमाविथdhacodamāvitha |

अरज्जौarajjau दस्यूनdasyūn समुनबsamunab दभीतयेdabhītaye सुप्राव्योभवःsuprāvyoabhavaḥ स.s. उ.u. ||
Thou boundest up the Dāsa's hundred friends and ten, when, at one's hearing, thou helpest thy worshipper.
Thou for Dabhīti boundest Dasyus not with cords; Thou wast a mighty help. Worthy of lauds art thou.
2.13.9
विश्वेदनुviśvedanu रोधनाrodhanā अस्यasya पौंस्यंpauṃsyaṃ ददुरस्मैdadurasmai दधिरेdadhire कर्त्नवेkṛtnave धनमdhanam |

षळṣaḷ अस्तभ्नाastabhnā विष्टिरःviṣṭiraḥ पञ्चpañca सन्द्र्शःsandṛśaḥ परिपरोpariparo अभवःabhavaḥ स.s. उ.u. ||
All banks of rivers yielded to his manly might; to him they gave, to him, the Strong, gave up their wealth.
The six directions hast thou fixed, a five-fold view: thy victories reached afar. Worthy of lauds art thou.
2.13.10
सुप्रवाचनंsupravācanaṃ तवtava वीरvīra वीर्यंvīryaṃ यदेकेनyadekena करतुनाkratunā विन्दसेvindase वसुvasu |

जातूष्ठिरस्यjātūṣṭhirasya परpra वयःvayaḥ सहस्वतोsahasvato याyā चकर्थcakartha सेन्द्रsendra विश्वास्युक्थ्यःviśvāsyukthyaḥ ||
Meet for high praise, O Hero, is thy power, that with thy single wisdom thou obtainest wealth,
The life-support of conquering Jātūṣṭhira. Indra, for all thy deeds, worthy of lauds art thou.
2.13.11
अरमयःaramayaḥ सरपसस्तरायsarapasastarāya कंkaṃ तुर्वीतयेturvītaye चca वय्यायvayyāya चca सरुतिमsrutim |

नीचाnīcā सन्तमुदनयःsantamudanayaḥ पराव्र्जंparāvṛjaṃ परान्धंprāndhaṃ शरोणंśroṇaṃ शरवयनśravayan स.s. उ.u. ||
Thou for Turvīti heldest still the flowing floods, the river-stream for Vayya easily to pass
Didst raise the outcast from the depths, and gavest fame unto the halt and blind. Worthy of lauds art thou.
2.13.12
अस्मभ्यंasmabhyaṃ तदtad वसोvaso दानायdānāya राधःrādhaḥ समर्थयस्वsamarthayasva बहुbahu तेte वसव्यमvasavyam |

इन्द्रindra यच्चित्रंyaccitraṃ शरवस्याśravasyā अनुanu दयूनdyūn बर्हदbṛhad वदेमvadema व.v. स.s. ||
Prepare thyself to grant us that great bounty, O Vasu, for abundant is thy treasure.
Snatch up the wonderful, O Indra, daily. Loud may we speak, with heroes, in assembly.
